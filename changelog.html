The effect: agency continuity is built into the constitutional mechanism rather than depending on a separate provision; the acting director problem does not arise; successive removal notices are less disruptive because the director keeps working throughout each window.

v149 May 2026 Minor
§15.1.c — Citizen-Led Amendment Re-Vote Provision

The exception clause in §15.1.c — which gave the Judicial Inspectorate a certification role incompatible with the information-only mandate — is replaced with an automatic re-vote mechanism. A near-miss with commanding affirmative support earns a second attempt at the full standard. The threshold never moves.

Constitutional Change
  • §15.1.cThe exception clause ("the JI may certify the amendment as presumptively valid") is replaced. New provision: if participation falls between 60-65% and the affirmative vote exceeds 75%, the Electoral Commission automatically schedules a second vote on the same amendment text — no sooner than 90 days and no later than 12 months after the first result is certified. The second vote uses the full 70%/65% standard. If the second vote also fails, the amendment fails and the same text may not trigger a further re-vote. No institution decides whether the re-vote is merited; the provision self-executes when the arithmetic conditions are met.
Rationale
  • WhyThe original exception gave the JI a "may certify" role — a binding decision — incompatible with §8.6's information-only mandate. The JI observes and reports; it does not certify constitutional amendments. The re-vote mechanism removes all institutional judgment from the exception: the arithmetic either meets the trigger conditions or it does not. The EC schedules the re-vote; the second vote uses the full standard; the threshold never moves. Near-misses with genuine support earn a second chance without the threshold being lowered for them.
Scenario Updated
  • The PetitionScenario rewritten to reflect the re-vote mechanism. First vote: 77.2% affirmative, 63.1% participation — re-vote triggered automatically. Eight months later: 74.8% affirmative, 64.1% participation — still 0.9% short. Amendment fails. The outcome shows the re-vote is a genuine test at the full standard, not a consolation path. The campaign improved participation by 1.0 point but lost 2.4 points of affirmative support — mobilizing broader participation diluted intensity among committed supporters.
