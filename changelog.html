Changelog

Every revision to the Federated Republic — what changed, why it changed, and what gap it closes. The constitution is a living document; this is its institutional memory.

About versioning. Version numbers track the constitutional document, not the website. A version increment means at least one provision was added, revised, or removed. Minor fixes — terminology corrections, cross-reference repairs, drafting errors — are included where they affect meaning. The document is available for download at each current version. Prior versions are archived in the project repository.
v122 May 2026 Current
Cross-Reference Audit · Article XIX Alignment · State Designee Panel

Full cross-reference audit identified ten internal errors accumulated across v119–v121 revisions. Article XIX (Ratification and Transition) updated to reflect the elected MA Director and the v120 submitted-record model. The Inspectorate Oversight Committee eliminated and replaced by a State Designee Panel — a rotating body of State-appointed representatives with the single function of verifying For-Cause IG removal grounds.

  • §2.1.g / §12.3Rule Violation cross-references corrected: §2.8 → §2.7. Rule Violations are governed by §2.7; §2.8 is Emergency Governance Protocol.
  • §2.4.a / §2.8 / §8.4.d / §12.2.eEmergency Governance Protocol cross-references corrected: §2.9 → §2.8 throughout. §2.9 is Clemency; Emergency Governance is §2.8.
  • §19.5Classification Framework reference corrected: §9.4 → §9.5, following the Emergency Mode renumbering in v121.
  • §19.9Missing section heading added. §19.9 (Transitional Accountability Mechanism) existed as a provision without a heading.
  • Schedule AUnamendable provisions list completed: §1.17, §1.18, §1.27 added alongside §1.2, §1.3, §1.13. All six permanently unamendable provisions now listed.
Article XIX — Transition Alignment

  • §19.8.aRegister assembly updated: MA Director → MA board members + RSA Director. Old proactive assessment model replaced with submitted-record model consistent with v120.
  • §19.8.bInterim functions updated: MA Director is elected (Phase 3), not confirmed. PM nominates MA board members within 90 days of Phase 4 activation; RSA Director within 180 days.
  • Phase 3 TableMA Director election added to the list of Phase 3 National Election Day offices.

Institutional Reform — State Designee Panel

  • §8.9 newState Designee Panel established. Each State and Territory maintains one standing designee. Five-member panel drawn by lot at each two-year election cycle. Single function: binary verification of For-Cause removal grounds. Compensated from federal budget during active service only. Panel completes work in progress at cycle end (max one additional year).

v120 May 2026 Major
Inspectorate Model Revision · Legislature Terminology

Two significant changes. First: the Inspectorate candidate assessment model was redesigned — nominating actors now submit candidate disclosure records; Inspectorates review for accuracy and publish recommendations rather than generating independent assessments. Second: "Parliament" and "Parliamentary" replaced throughout with "Legislature" and "Legislative", reflecting the document's universal applicability rather than a British parliamentary association.

Inspectorate Assessment Model
  • §8.1.bEI mandate description updated: reviews submitted candidate disclosure records, publishes recommendations. Does not generate independent assessments.
  • §8.3.bExecutive IG appointment: House submits candidate disclosure record to JI (not EI); JI reviews and publishes recommendation before Senate confirmation.
  • §8.4.aQualified Jurist Registry: candidates submit registration records; JI reviews for accuracy and eligibility; publishes recommendation. SC appeals under §8.9.d unchanged.
  • §8.6.g newCitizen Discrepancy Petition added. Any citizen who identifies a discrepancy in a published candidate disclosure record may petition their elected representative. No fee, threshold, or filing requirement.
  • §4.2.cTriad nominee vetting: Registry record serves as the disclosure record. JI confirms Registry status and no disqualifying change since admission — does not generate a new assessment.
  • §5.4.b / §9.3 / §10.4.a / §17.4.gAll PM-nominated positions updated: PM submits candidate disclosure record to EI; EI reviews and publishes recommendation. Old proactive assessment model removed.
  • §16.2Electoral Commission candidates submit disclosure records. EI reviews factual accuracy; JI separately applies eligibility criteria. Both published to NRS before election.
Terminology
  • Throughout"Parliament" → "the Legislature" and "Parliamentary" → "Legislative" in all uses referring to the Republic's legislature. Government-system descriptors ("parliamentary vs presidential") preserved unchanged. 186 run-level changes in the constitutional document; 189 changes across the website.
v119 May 2026 Major
Presidential Removal · Triad Revision · Judicial Overhaul · MA Board Model

The most structurally significant revision since the document's founding. Fourteen distinct amendments across Articles II, III, IV, V, IX, X, XVI, and XVII. Four areas of focus: presidential accountability (two new removal tracks), Supreme Court nomination (Triad redesigned), judicial removal and vacancy (three-track architecture for all courts), and Monetary Authority governance (elected Director plus board with veto/override).

Article II — Presidential Accountability
  • §2.7.e newRule Violation modified consequences for legislators: suspension from voting and committee participation; mandatory §3.6 expulsion referral within 30 days. Chambers retain the expulsion decision but cannot avoid commencing proceedings.
  • §2.10 newPresidential removal — two political tracks independent of §2.7. Path 1: 2/3 House to initiate + 2/3 Senate to convict (Statement of Grounds required). Path 2: Senate simple majority refers to national recall referendum; removal requires majority of eligible voters; one referral per term.
  • §2.2.g newMinisterial appointments defined: PM appoints and removes ministers within the Domestic Affairs Council; all appointments published to NRS; ministers exercise only authority delegated within the PM's domain.
Article III — Legislative Accountability
  • §3.2.h newSenate Speaker vacancy: Deputy Speaker assumes succession role immediately; Senate must elect new Speaker within 72 hours. Protects the presidential succession line.
  • §3.5Scope clarified: §3.5 impeachment applies exclusively to executive and judicial officers. Legislative members governed exclusively by §3.6.
  • §3.6.eExpanded: States and Territories may establish recall mechanisms for both Senators and House members.
  • §3.6.f newVacancy filling: States and Territories determine replacement process by their own statute. Legislature sets maximum vacancy period — no seat may remain indefinitely unfilled.
Article IV — Judicial Architecture
  • §4.1.bInferior court vacancy: 180-day constitutional ceiling. PM has 90 days to nominate; if fails, Supreme Court nominates from Qualified Jurist Registry. Deemed Confirmed prevents House inaction veto. Legislature-defined confirmation period cannot push past ceiling.
  • §4.1.cInferior court judge removal: three independent tracks — Rule Violation (§2.7), judicial initiation (2/3 SC petition + legislative supermajority), political removal (mirroring presidential tracks). §3.5 explicitly excluded.
  • §4.2 TriadSenate replaces House as Triad nominator. Senate/PM/President each nominate one candidate from Qualified Jurist Registry. Senate eligibility confirmation round eliminated — JI Registry vetting is the sole eligibility filter. All certified nominees proceed directly to national RCV election.
  • §4.2.b newMid-term vacancy process: President and PM nominate; Senate has 90 days for third nominee. If Senate fails, two-nominee public election. Replacement serves remainder of original term.
  • §4.2.c / §4.2.dJI vetting and public election provisions revised to reflect new Triad and Registry model.
  • §4.2.d Emergency PanelRotation rules simplified: "at least two members of the sitting Panel must have served on the prior cycle" replaces the 18-month minimum and two-retained-justices rules, resolving a mid-cycle retirement edge case.
  • §4.2 political removalSupreme Court justices: political removal mirrors presidential tracks. 2/3 both chambers or Senate referral to popular recall. For-Cause only; JI verification required before Legislature votes.
Article X — Monetary Authority
  • §10.4.aMA Director is now elected by national RCV on National Election Day to a single non-renewable 7-year term — the only administrative officer directly elected by citizens. Five board members nominated by PM from Candidate Register, Senate-confirmed on staggered terms.
  • §10.4 veto/overrideDirector may veto any board determination. Board may override Director's veto by 4-of-5 vote. Both veto and override reasoning published to NRS immediately. Applies to all board determinations including BNF certifications.
  • §10.4.bIndependence provision updated: any Director or board member who exercises veto or override under political direction commits a Rule Violation.
Other Articles
  • §9.3 / §9.4RSA Director: 7-year term defined; removal threshold established; §9.3 Emergency Mode renamed §9.4 to resolve duplicate numbering.
  • §5.4.b / §17.4.gCLT Administrator and National Trust Administrator: confirmed officer removal thresholds defined; mid-term vacancy provisions added; explicit eligibility criteria added for CLT Administrator.
  • §16.5.d newElectoral Commission mid-term vacancy: special election within 90 days for popular seats; joint legislative session within 90 days for legislative seats. Quorum defined; odd-number requirement preserved.
Gap this revision closes: Prior to v119, the document had no political removal mechanism for the President beyond the §2.7 Rule Violation track, no defined process for mid-term Supreme Court vacancies, and a Triad that effectively gave the PM two nominations (House + PM). It also lacked explicit removal thresholds for several constitutional officers and had no vacancy provisions for inferior court seats. v119 addresses all of these simultaneously.
v118 April 2026 Baseline
Session Baseline — Twenty Articles, 766 Paragraphs

The version of the document at the start of the current drafting session. Twenty articles covering individual rights, the dual executive, the legislature, the judicial architecture, citizenship, immigration, elections, the inspectorates, the national record system, public finance, direct democracy, national defense, territorial governance, indigenous compacts, amendment procedures, the electoral commission, federal property, electoral finance, ratification, and associated communities.

Document at Baseline
  • Articles I–XXFull constitutional framework. Twenty articles, 766 paragraphs, 282 interactive provisions in the annotated version.
  • §1.25.eElections cannot be postponed under any emergency. Any such order is automatically void.
  • §2.3.bConstructive vote of no confidence: the House can only remove the PM by simultaneously electing a named successor. Destructive oppositions cannot manufacture governmental instability.
  • §2.7Rule Violation automatic consequences: Supreme Court finding triggers immediate suspension, access revocation, and Senate trial — no vote required, no discretion available.
  • §6.1Dual-gate immigration: State sponsorship (Gate 1) and federal certification (Gate 2). No nationality quotas. Statistical disparity presumption protects against indirect discrimination.
  • §9.1National Record System: all executive orders, legislation, court decisions, and inspectorate findings published permanently. Unrecorded executive orders are void.
  • §15.3Six permanently unamendable provisions: §1.2 (torture), §1.3 (slavery), §1.13 (habeas corpus), §1.17 (public trial), §1.18 (retroactive laws), §1.27 (non-refoulement).
