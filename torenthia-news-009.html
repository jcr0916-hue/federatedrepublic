One on One · Civic Consul · Domestic Affairs

'The Domestic Side Has a Clock Too'

Civic Consul Nadia Sorrel sits down with TNB Caldenmere's Lenne Akar — a conversation eight years in the making — on the fiscal picture behind Lake Varda, the EM's Korda file, and what the next Legat Consul is going to inherit.

Lenne Akar TNB Caldenmere · Political Correspondent · Verentum — Year 12, Month 12

Nadia Sorrel chose this interview. That is the first thing to understand about it. She did not call RNN. She did not give The Torenthian a statement. She called TNB Caldenmere, where I covered her first Assembly campaign eight years ago from a folding chair in the back of a Caldenmere town meeting. When I asked her why, she said: "Lenne has been asking me real questions since I was a backbencher. I knew I'd get real questions."

We met in her working office in Verentum — not the formal reception room, the office with the papers on the desk — on a Tuesday afternoon, eleven days after the Lake Varda incident. She had the NRS-Y12-3089 notification on the desk when I arrived. She did not move it.

Lenne Akar

Everyone is talking about the eastern border. The candidates are talking about it, RNN is talking about it. What are you talking about?

CC Sorrel

I'm talking about three hundred and forty thousand Republic Units in coast guard expenditure above the standing appropriation. I'm talking about seven people at Varenne Station whose legal status hasn't been determined and whose medical costs are sitting on the federal emergency health account. I'm talking about a Korda Territory fiscal file that the Executive Monitor has been reviewing since before any of this happened. None of that is secret. All of it is in the NRS.

Lenne Akar

Is it a budget crisis?

CC Sorrel

No. Not yet. The reserves are adequate for the current expenditure. What it is, is a clock. The EM has the Korda Territory file. The Legislature has ninety days to respond to any finding the EM publishes. I would prefer to have a response ready before the ninety days runs. That means I need to have a conversation with the Assembly about what Korda Territory's fiscal position actually looks like — not after an EM finding forces the question, but before.

"The domestic side has a clock too. It just doesn't run as fast as an incident, so people don't notice it until it's already late."

Lenne Akar

The LC candidates are all running on foreign policy. On the eastern border, on Sunderland, on what the Republic's posture should be. Is anyone talking to you about what you just described?

CC Sorrel

They're running for Legat Consul. Their domain is foreign affairs and defense. I wouldn't expect them to be running on the fiscal equalization mechanism. That's my domain. What I would say is this: whoever wins that race is going to sit down with a Civic Consul who has been working this file since Month 12. That's not a threat. That's what continuity looks like. It should matter to them how well that handoff goes.

Lenne Akar

Legat Consul Rehn briefed you formally under §2.5. What did that look like?

CC Sorrel

The NRS record says what it says. There was also a classified component that I'm not going to characterize. What I will say is that Rehn runs a thorough process. We don't always agree on sequencing — that's the nature of a dual executive — but we agree on what the constitution requires. He notified me when he was required to. I acknowledged it and started working. That's how it's supposed to function.

Lenne Akar

You requested an interim EM review of Korda Territory's social state infrastructure. That's in the NRS acknowledgment. Why interim rather than waiting for the annual review?

CC Sorrel

Because seven people are at Varenne Station right now. Because there are more people attempting to cross Lake Varda than there were in Month 10. Because the annual review was designed for ordinary conditions, and these are not ordinary conditions. The EM has the authority to conduct interim reviews. I asked. That's the mechanism working the way it's supposed to work.

Lenne Akar

You've been Civic Consul since Year 10. You've watched this Republic navigate a lot of things. What do you think people are missing about this moment?

CC Sorrel

The same thing they always miss. The foreign affairs story is visible. The Legat Consul issues diplomatic notes, candidates give speeches, RNN has a banner on the screen. The domestic side doesn't look like that. It looks like a spreadsheet and a ninety-day clock and a conversation with the Assembly Finance Committee that nobody is going to broadcast. But it matters just as much. The domestic side has a clock too. It just doesn't run as fast as an incident, so people don't notice it until it's already late.

Civic Consul Sorrel did not announce any future political plans during this interview. The TNB Caldenmere interview aired in full on the regional broadcast and is available in the TNB digital archive.

Lenne Akar is TNB Caldenmere's political correspondent. She has covered Caldenmere State and federal politics since Year 3.
