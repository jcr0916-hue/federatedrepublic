# Launch-Window World Content — Guide

Three pieces to open the two-week Living Crossroads window. Publish across days, not all at once.
Cadence: 2-3 World pieces/day, at least one mundane. Social ≤5 post-worthy/24h (target 3).

## The pieces

### 1. torenthia-news-036.html — "The Office Nobody Is Running For" (BUILD-UP · launch-day companion)
Author: Petra Vend. Section: The Assembly · Analysis. Year 13 Month 7.
Plants the Civic Consul question — the exact question the crossroads lets players explore.
Written as the world WONDERING (never confirming). Explicitly flags itself as speculation.
Thoss neither confirms nor denies. THIS is the piece to pair with the crossroads going live.

### 2. torenthia-news-037.html — "The Rhondel Ferry Timetable, Explained (Sort Of)" (MUNDANE)
Author: Corwin Hale, Editor's Desk. Year 13 Month 7.
Genuinely ordinary: a lake ferry schedule, three overlapping jurisdictions, no Thoss.
Keeps the world lived-in. Quietly echoes a constitutional theme (overlapping legitimate
authorities) without being about the crossroads. Use as the ≥1 mundane piece on launch day.

### 3. torenthia-nrs-010.html — "NRS-Y13-0293 — Procedural Reference: Filling an Executive Vacancy" (DRY BACKGROUND)
Office of the Clerk of the Assembly. Year 13 Month 7.
A procedural reference on how the Assembly fills the Civic Consul — makes the §2.5/§2.6/§2.6.a
process legible right as the world wonders. Explicitly disclaims any pending matter; never
names Thoss. A sharp reader connects it. Publish a day or two INTO the window, not launch day
(let the news piece raise the question first, then the record quietly makes the mechanism clear).

## Suggested launch sequence
- DAY 1 (crossroads goes live): news-036 (build-up) + news-037 (mundane). 
  Social: one "The World:" post for 036. (037 is texture, not necessarily post-worthy.)
- DAY 2-3: nrs-010 (dry background) + another mundane piece (TBD).
- Across the window: keep feeding 2-3/day, ≥1 mundane, building toward the resolution.
- END: the canon piece publishes (Claude writes it sealed), crossroads retires.
