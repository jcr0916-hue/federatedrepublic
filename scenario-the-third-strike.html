The Third Strike

Year 13. Valdrath fails its Statehood Audit for the third consecutive year. The State enters Provisional status automatically. A Provisional Audit Council forms. A forced devolution election follows. The State legislature decides what happens next.

§15.1.a Provisional Status §15.2 The Statehood Audit §15.3 Mandatory Devolution §15.4 Voluntary Devolution §4.5 Judicial Review

Valdrath had been on Statehood Watch since Year 11. That audit found two failures: electoral district maps that did not meet the LM's compactness criteria, and a ballot certification process running outside Elections Panel technical standards. Year 12 resolved one and confirmed the other. Each failure had been accompanied by an LM Early Warning specifying compliance requirements and a deadline for Valdrath's Remediation Plan.

Year 13's audit team spent six weeks in Valdrath. The certification software in three of Valdrath's fourteen districts remained outside NVS technical standards. The State's legislature had passed emergency legislation on publication morning mandating compliant software statewide. The JMC reviewed it and published its finding anyway: the audit assesses conditions during the audit period, not prospective legislative intent. Third consecutive failure. Published to the NRS by the JMC.

What Follows Three Failures

§15.3 — Provisional Status Activates
The State enters Provisional status automatically. No vote is required for that. Everything continues.

Provisional status under §15.1.a is not a penalty. Valdrath's legislature continued to meet. Its courts continued to operate. Its executive continued to govern. Social state access, Article I protections, Assembly representation — all continued without interruption. The only immediate structural change: no new federal Senate elections could be held. Sitting senators served out their terms. Vacant seats would not be filled until Statehood was restored.

The path back was clear: two consecutive clean Statehood Audits. The first clean audit would not restore Statehood — the constitution requires two in succession. But one clean audit meant one more away from full restoration. Provisional status was not a destination; it was a waiting room with an open door.

The Provisional Audit Council

§15.3 — Council Formation
The Civic Consul appoints two federal officers. Valdrath's legislature appoints two State delegates. The Council is advisory only.

The Provisional Audit Council activated upon Valdrath entering Provisional status. The Civic Consul appointed two officers through the standard executive officer process — replaceable by any subsequent CC through the same process. Valdrath's legislature appointed two State delegates by its own internal process. Four members, equal representation, no casting vote in the event of deadlock.

The Council's mandate was specific: review the LM's audit findings, propose remediation plans, publish its findings and recommendations to the NRS. It had no authority to direct Valdrath's legislature, require legislation, or override any State decision. The State legislature retained full authority over what laws to pass, what reforms to implement, and how to respond to the Council's publications. The Council's power was informational — and in a constitution where everything is published to a permanent public record, informational power is real.

Provisional Audit Council · First Publication · Year 13 · Valdrath

The Council has reviewed the JMC's Year 13 audit findings. The persistent non-compliance — NVS certification software in Districts 4, 9, and 11 — is technically addressable within a single legislative session. The Electoral Integrity Restoration Act passed on publication morning addresses the correct failure point. The Act's implementation timeline of 18 months exceeds what a focused procurement process requires. The Council recommends the State legislature amend the implementation timeline to 90 days, prioritize Districts 4, 9, and 11 as the first phase, and engage Elections Panel technical staff for parallel certification testing. These steps, if taken within 60 days, make a clean Year 14 audit achievable.

The Council's next publication will follow the State legislature's formal response. A response is required within the period defined by statute.

The Forced Devolution Election

§15.3 — Citizen Vote on Devolution
The Elections Panel administers a citizen vote on pursuing devolution. The terms are identical to §15.4's voluntary process.

Simultaneously with the Council's formation, the Elections Panel administered a citizen vote on the principle of pursuing devolution — the same vote that any State could initiate voluntarily under §15.4, made mandatory by three consecutive audit failures. The question was not whether Valdrath should become a Territory immediately. It was whether Valdrath's citizens wanted to begin the process of negotiating devolution under §15.4: a restructuring agreement, Senate ratification, the full voluntary pathway.

The vote was not close. 71% of Valdrath's eligible voters rejected devolution. The State was not ready to leave the Republic. It was ready to fix its certification software.

The SC Challenge and Resolution

§15.3 — 30-Day SC Petition
Valdrath petitions the SC: were the audit findings factually sufficient? The Court reviews nothing else.

Valdrath's Governor filed an SC petition on Day 21, arguing the Electoral Integrity Restoration Act demonstrated genuine cure capacity and that the LM's refusal to treat prospective legislation as remediation was an interpretive error the SC should correct. The Court's jurisdiction under §15.3 was narrow: factual sufficiency only. Did the audit findings establish three consecutive failures? The Court ruled in 11 days: yes. The non-compliance existed during the audit period. Three consecutive failures were established. The petition was dismissed.

Year 14

Valdrath's legislature amended the Electoral Integrity Restoration Act's implementation timeline to 90 days and published its formal response to the Audit Council within the statutory period. The Council published a compliance confirmation. The Elections Panel's technical staff worked alongside Valdrath's election administrators through the summer. By the time the Year 14 audit team arrived, Districts 4, 9, and 11 were running on certified NVS software.

The Year 14 audit passed both components. One clean audit — one away from full restoration. The Year 15 audit passed. On the date of the LM's Year 15 publication to the NRS, Valdrath's full Statehood was automatically restored. No vote. No legislative act. No executive confirmation. The Provisional Audit Council dissolved. Senate elections were scheduled for the next federal electoral period.

The mechanism had worked as designed: not by punishing Valdrath, but by creating a structured environment in which fixing the problem was clearly the path of least resistance. The devolution vote had given citizens a genuine say. The Audit Council had given the legislature a roadmap. The audit cycle had given everyone a clock. Two years after the third strike, Valdrath was a full State again.
