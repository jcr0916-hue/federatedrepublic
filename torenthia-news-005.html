Rhovane Town Hall: Mak Tells Constituents 'This City Is Carrying Too Much' — Crowd Sees More Than a Local Meeting

Assembly member seen as potential LC candidate addresses packed hall in Vardenmere. Does not declare. Does not deflect.

RNN Digital · Vardenmere, Rhovane — Year 12, Month 11

VARDENMERE, RHOVANE — Assembly member Seren Mak did not announce a run for Legat Consul on Tuesday night. She also did not deny one. What she did was stand in front of four hundred constituents in a packed community hall in Vardenmere and tell them, in careful and specific language, that Rhondel cannot keep absorbing a regional crisis without a constitutional response from Verentum.

The crowd, by all accounts, heard something more.

"This is not a Rhovane problem. This is a Republic problem, and the Republic has not yet decided to treat it like one."

The town hall, billed as a constituent services meeting, drew attendees from across the state — including, several in the room noted, faces not typically seen at Rhovane events. Mak did not address her political future directly. When a constituent asked whether she intended to run, she smiled and said she was there to talk about Vardenmere. The room laughed. The questioner sat down looking satisfied.

The Specifics

Mak spent the first forty minutes on specifics. Asylum Court overflow in Rhondel is now in its second year of "temporary" operation. Coast guard capacity in Vardenmere harbor has not been materially increased since Year 9. The refugee intake infrastructure in northern Korda Territory — the primary arrival zone for those crossing Lake Varda — is operating above designed capacity and drawing on State resources Rhovane did not budget for.

"When Verentum looks at this situation, they see a foreign affairs file," Mak said. "When I stand in Vardenmere, I see an infrastructure problem, a housing problem, an Asylum Court problem, and a coast guard problem. Those are not foreign. Those are ours."

She cited three NRS-published petitions from Rhondel to Verentum in the past eighteen months. She did not editorialize about the response. She did not need to.

On Sunderland

On the question of Sunderland and the Lake Varda vessel incursions, Mak was notably precise. She praised the LC's office for publishing each diplomatic note to the NRS — "the record matters," she said — and said nothing further about how those notes had been received. The restraint was, in its way, as pointed as anything she said about infrastructure.

Mak represents a Rhovane district that runs along the lake's western shore. Her constituents fish the lake, crew the harbor, and staff the coast guard installation at Vardenmere's north end. They track the vessel incursions the way people track weather — not because they can change it, but because it determines what they can do.

The Field

Senator Tobias Vael of Harren declared for Legat Consul last month, making him the first in the field. Director of Foreign Affairs Ines Carrow addressed the Verentum Forum two weeks ago with remarks many observers read as a de facto campaign address; she has not declared. Mak has not declared.

She left the hall to a standing ovation.

Outside, asked by RNN whether Tuesday night felt like the beginning of something, a Vardenmere city councillor who attended said: "Ask me that again in thirty days."
