NRS-Y13-0291
Redacted Intelligence Summary — Varda Crossing Incursion
Record Identification
NRS Reference:NRS-Y13-0291
Record Type:Redacted Intelligence Summary — Public Disclosure
Publishing Authority:Foreign Affairs Directorate of the Federated Republic
Filed By:Ines Carrow, Director of Foreign Affairs
Subject:Month 6 armed incursion, Lake Varda approach, Korda Territory
Related Records:NRS-Y13-0233 (Sunderland Inquiry); Legat Consul's Note of Protest, Month 5 Day 8
Classification Basis:Temporary confidentiality applied to source-identifying detail only; §10.3, with §1.19.e ceiling
Date of Publication:Year 13, Month 6
Purpose of Disclosure
This summary is published so that the public and the Legislature possess a common, verified account of the Varda Crossing incursion rather than a contested one. Where a matter touching the Republic's security can be disclosed without endangering the sources or methods by which it was learned, the Directorate's practice is to disclose it. What follows has been redacted only to the extent necessary to protect source-identifying detail; the redactions are marked, and their basis is stated. Nothing is withheld permanently, and nothing material to public understanding of what occurred has been withheld at all.
Verified Account
On the morning in question, an armed Sunderland formation of approximately company strength, mounted in ████████████ vehicles, crossed the Korda border marker at the Lake Varda approach and advanced roughly four hundred meters onto Republic ground. The formation remained for nine minutes. During that period an exchange of fire occurred; two Korda border personnel were killed and one was wounded. The formation withdrew across the marker following ████████████████████. The Directorate assesses with high confidence that the crossing was executed by a standing Sunderland unit and not by an irregular or unaffiliated group.
The Attribution Question
The Directorate assesses — with moderate confidence, and this is the central judgment of this summary — that the formation acted ████████ the direct operational control of the Sunderland Interim Authority's central command at the time of the crossing. The distinction the Directorate sought in its Month 5 inquiry (NRS-Y13-0233) — between a crossing directed by an identifiable authorized command and one occurring outside command control — is therefore not resolved in the Interim Authority's favor. A command that cannot account for a company-strength unit conducting a lethal cross-border operation is either not in control of that unit or declining to say so. The Directorate does not, on the present evidence, know which.
What This Summary Does Not Claim
This summary does not establish who fired first. It does not attribute intent to the Interim Authority's central leadership. It does not recommend a Republic response; the authorization and allocation of any military response rests with the Legislature under §14.2 and with the Legat Consul in office, and this Directorate holds neither authority. It is a record of what is known, published so that what is known is shared.
This summary is published under the Directorate's ordinary authority to inform the public and the Legislature on matters of the Republic's foreign relations. The confidentiality applied to the marked passages is temporary and source-protective; it carries no permanent seal, and the Directorate has filed the unredacted summary with the Executive Monitor for compliance review under §9.1.b.

This is not a campaign communication and is not subject to the electoral finance disclosure requirements applicable to campaign materials under §8.1. The Directorate's conduct of this matter is unaffected by the Director's status as a declared candidate for Legat Consul, and the same summary would have been published on the same day had she not been a candidate at all.

The Republic is entitled to know what its government knows. It knows this now.
Published to the National Record System under Article X. Temporary source-protective redactions are marked and are subject to the §1.19.e prohibition on permanent withholding; the Executive Monitor's compliance review will be published to the NRS in the ordinary course.
