Attendance

Convened jointly under §2.14. Both executives present.

Legat Consul:Casimir Rehn
Civic Consul:Elin Thoss

Domain officers of the Legat Consul: all seated, unchanged from the preceding record.

Ministers of the Civic Consul: first attendance of the ministers appointed at NRS-Y13-0336. Territorial Infrastructure (Rews), Fiscal Administration (Casta), and nine further portfolios attending; Health (Verel) attending, retained from the preceding Council.

The Secretariat notes for the record that four portfolios remain filled on an acting basis pending appointment and that acting officers attend with full participation.

Matters Considered

The Council considered four matters crossing executive domains. The Council makes recommendations and coordinates positions; it does not exercise executive authority, and nothing in this record effects any act.

Item 1 — Lake Varda Corridor: Continuity of Arrival Processing

Domestic reception and processing at the lake corridor sit in the Civic Consul's domain; the coast-guard and border posture on the water sits in the Legat Consul's. The item concerned nothing more than continuity: whether the change of ministers alters any standing coordination arrangement at Varenne Station.

Position coordinated: no arrangement lapses on a change of minister. Existing liaison at station level continues unchanged. The Council recommends the two offices restate the arrangement in writing within thirty days, the Secretariat noting that the current understanding predates both sitting ministers and exists chiefly in the practice of officers who have since rotated.

Item 2 — Sunderland Interim Authority: Inquiry Status

Raised by the Legat Consul's domain. The standing inquiry regarding the Year 13 crossings remains unanswered by the Sunderland Interim Authority. No new communication has been received since the acknowledgment of receipt.

The item was recorded and no recommendation was made. Foreign communication is enumerated to the Legat Consul under §2.1; the Council received the report and did not deliberate on the response, that being outside its function.

Item 3 — Fiscal Review Notice: Coordination of Departmental Submissions

Raised by the Civic Consul's domain. Following the notice of intended legislation regarding the §12.8 mechanism, several offices in both domains will be asked to provide submissions to the legislative committee.

Position coordinated: submissions are provided to the committee directly by the office holding the material, in each case published to the NRS on transmission. The Council declined to establish a central clearing point for submissions. The record notes the Civic Consul's stated reason: that a mechanism reviewed in public should not have its evidence assembled in private first, and that a submission routed through a coordinating office is a submission that has been edited by one.

The Legat Consul concurred as to his domain's submissions.

Item 4 — Assistance Request Under §2.14.b: Disposition

The standing request for transport and logistics support to the northern arrival zone (Norvane), lodged under §2.14.b at NRS-Y13-0298, reaches the end of its stated 90-day duration in Month 11.

The Secretariat records that assistance under §2.14.b may be renewed only by a fresh request and a fresh response, each published, and that no renewal is automatic. The Civic Consul's office indicated an intention to lodge a fresh request in Month 10 if the requirement continues. The Legat Consul's domain made no commitment as to the response, the Council noting for the record that it may not make one: the responding Consul determines whether and how to act, and neither Consul may compel the other.

Next Session

Ordinary session scheduled for Year 13, Month 10. The existence and general subject of every session are unclassifiable under §2.14 and appear in this record accordingly.

End of summary record.
