The Voluntary Federation

Alduria, Mervane, and Korsten have been economically integrated for twenty years through treaties none of them can constitutionally compel each other to honour. The Federated Republic is the constitutional completion of what has already happened. Three sovereign governments dissolve themselves simultaneously. Three legal traditions, three political cultures, three predecessor fiscal histories — and no crisis requiring resolution. The most different scenario in the series.

§19 Transition Architecture§2.1–2.2 Dual Executive§3.2 Senate§13.5 Associated Community§9 NRS Architecture§15.4 Independence Framework

Every scenario in this series tests how the Federated Republic handles a nation choosing its constitutional framework under some form of pressure — crisis, failure, rupture, or reform. The Voluntary Federation tests something different: can the document be adopted not as a rescue but as an achievement? Can three functioning democracies choose it together because the constitutional relationship is the honest completion of an economic and political integration that has already happened?

Setting — The Aldric Republic

Three nations share a peninsula on the northeastern edge of a major continental landmass. Alduria (population 2.4 million), Mervane (population 3.1 million), and Korsten (population 2.2 million) have been functionally integrated for two decades: a common currency, a customs union, open internal borders, and a joint defense command that has made their separate military establishments redundant in everything except national symbolism. What they do not have is a constitutional relationship. The integration is treaty-based — each parliament ratifies separately, each can in theory withdraw. The currency union's stability depends on three central banks coordinating policy none can individually compel. The joint defense command requires unanimous consent. The friction has been building for a decade.

The convention meets in the border city of Varenholm — a city whose eastern districts are Aldurian and western districts are Mervanish, whose residents hold both national citizenships, and whose existence as a divided city is the most visible symbol of what the integration has accomplished and what it has not yet resolved. Three delegations of exactly equal size. Three sovereign governments. A constitutional text that is simultaneously a founding document and a treaty of union.

Alduria: 63.4% on 71.2% participation. Mervane: 58.7% on 68.9% participation. Korsten: 61.1% on 70.4% participation. All three pass. The Aldric Republic is constituted.

What Makes the Founding Different

Three Predecessor Governments, Not One

The §19.6 pre-existing obligations provisions assume a single predecessor government. The Aldric Republic has three. The treaty of union specifies how their respective debts, treaty obligations, and legal inheritances are assumed — but the constitutional architecture had to adapt. The Day Zero certification covers three simultaneous governmental transitions. The MA's first debt certification is a consolidated assessment of three separate national debt positions. The Day Zero NRS publication — three nations' complete modern governmental archives simultaneously published — is the largest single constitutional publication event in any scenario. In a founding where no predecessor government has anything to hide from the others, the founding-period record is the most complete of any Republic scenario.

No Dominant Political Tradition

Alduria's parliamentary tradition is Westminster-derived. Mervane's is continental — proportional representation, coalition government, powerful constitutional court. Korsten's is semi-presidential. The dual executive is therefore received differently by each founding State. For Mervane, the PM as domestic executive is familiar. For Alduria, the directly elected President with authority over foreign affairs is a novelty. For Korsten, the domain separation resolves an ambiguity their prior semi-presidential system never cleanly settled — their founding generation immediately understands what the Emergency Panel is for because they had been arguing about that boundary for thirty years without a mechanism for resolving it.

The Senate Question — Acute From Day One

Mervane has 39% of the combined population. Alduria 31%. Korsten 30%. Equal State representation means Korsten has exactly the same Senate weight as Mervane. The Mervanish delegation spends three convention weeks arguing for population-weighted Senate representation. The Aldurian and Korsteni delegations resist. The resolution is the existing architecture: equal State representation, full stop. The Mervanish delegation accepts and signs. Their senators become the most constitutionally active in the Senate's first decade — filing more constitutional challenges than any other State's delegation, developing federalism jurisprudence through aggressive litigation. Equal representation accepted democratically produces the Republic's most engaged Senate actors.

Year 1-3 — The Founding Period

The First Presidential Election

Candidates must campaign across three linguistic communities, three political cultures, three sets of voter expectations. The combined score's regional breadth component immediately advantages candidates with cross-State appeal. The first President is a Mervanish constitutional lawyer whose academic work on federalism made her known across all three nations before the convention. Her combined score — 61.3% national, winning all three founding States — reflects genuine transnational legitimacy. The first PM, nominated by the largest House coalition, is Aldurian. The dual executive's first year produces 11 Emergency Panel referrals as the President and PM develop working doctrine — not hostile cohabitation but genuine institutional learning.

The Varenholm Problem

Varenholm functions as one city — one public transit system, one water infrastructure, one municipal culture — constitutionally divided between two States. Different State laws apply to residents in the same neighborhoods. State tax rates differ. State education curricula diverge. Parliament's Year 4 Varenholm Act establishes a joint State administrative commission for specific municipal functions. The Supreme Court upholds it 5-4. The Act produces the Aldric Republic's most significant jurisprudential contribution: the doctrine that constitutional architecture sets minimums, not maximums, for sub-State governance innovation.

Year 4-15 — Consolidation and the Verdict

The Language Rights Cases

A significant Mervanish-speaking minority in Korsten petitions for official State recognition of Mervanish. The State declines. The Supreme Court's Year 5 ruling: States have full authority over their official administrative languages. The Court's Year 6 ruling on the §1.8 national origin challenge — 5-4, the most contested of the Republic's first decade — holds that formally neutral language policy does not violate §1.8 even where it produces differential practical burdens on linguistic minorities. The dissent argues this is precisely what §1.8 was designed to prevent. Both opinions are on the NRS. The debate continues at Year 15.

The Integration Dividend

By Year 10, the combined economy is approximately 12% larger than the three founding nations' economies would have been separately — the MA's certified integration dividend from full constitutional union over treaty-based integration. The dividend is unevenly distributed: Mervane benefits most in absolute terms; Korsten most in per-capita terms. The fiscal distribution formula — Mervane's net contributor argument versus Korsten's constitutional federalism argument — is the Republic's most persistent political debate, documented in complete detail on the NRS, unresolved at Year 15 because the constitution correctly leaves it to democratic politics.

The First Associated Community — The Island

In Year 14, a small island community (population 8,400) historically under Aldurian sovereignty completes the independence process at 71.2% on 66.4% participation. Within six months, its traditional council government initiates compact negotiations under §13.5. The JI pre-ratification certification finds no systematic Article I violations. The compact is ratified. The island becomes the Aldric Republic's first Associated Community — defense and BNF protections from the Republic, complete governance autonomy preserved including its hereditary council structure, an observing delegate in the House. A community that wanted to leave has found a constitutional status that lets it remain connected without being subsumed. The compact is its own. The sovereignty is real.

The Most Important Finding

The Voluntary Federation is the scenario where the document's bet is most clearly visible. The Aldric Republic chose the Federated Republic not because its prior constitutional arrangements were broken but because the constitutional relationship was the honest completion of an integration that had already happened. The document can be adopted for its architecture rather than as a rescue. Three sovereign nations chose it together. Their complete governmental records are on the NRS permanently. The integration dividend is documented and real. The Emergency Panel's 47 rulings in eight years are the architecture building constitutional doctrine from genuine uncertainty rather than from adversarial exploitation.

What remains open at Year 15: the language rights question, the fiscal distribution debate, and one gap the scenario exposed — the document has no explicit multi-founder constitutional establishment pathway. The transition provisions assume one predecessor government. Improvisation filled the gap. Improvisation that works once should become architecture.
