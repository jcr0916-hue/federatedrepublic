The Audit

Year 3. A Territory passes both components of the Statehood Audit — LM fiscal integrity, JM Article I compliance. No political vote required. The pathway is automatic. What happens next, in what order, and who can stop it.

§15.2 The Statehood Audit§9.1.a Legislative Monitor§9.1.c Judicial Monitor§3.5 The Senate

The Territory of Velmora had been working toward Statehood for four years. It had submitted its first Statehood Audit request in Year 1. That audit had failed — the JM found two areas of Article I non-compliance, both in the Territory's criminal procedure statutes. Velmora published its remediation plan and spent fourteen months addressing the findings.

The second audit was submitted in Year 3. Both components passed: the LM certified fiscal integrity; the JM certified full Article I compliance. The results were published to the NRS. The audits had been clean. Nothing remained to be voted on. The question was only what happened next — automatically, and in what order.

§15.2 — What Is Automatic
No political vote is required once the audit passes. The pathway is automatic.

The Statehood Audit passing is not a recommendation to the Legislature. It is a constitutional trigger. Upon both components passing, Velmora entered a Provisional Membership Period — the duration set by the Legislature at between one and three years. The Legislature had previously defined the standard period as eighteen months. That period began from the date of audit certification, not from any subsequent legislative action.

During the Provisional Membership Period, Velmora held full rights and was subject to annual ongoing audits. Assembly seats continued on the same proportional basis. There were no Senate seats yet — those were reserved for States. The Civic Consul's office coordinated the transition framework. No Senate ratification, no executive signature, no political vote of any kind was constitutionally required.

§15.2 — Clean Completion
At the end of the Provisional Period, automatic Statehood. No further action required.

Eighteen months later, the annual audit certified clean. Velmora's Statehood took effect automatically upon LM publication of the clean provisional period audit to the NRS. Two Senate seats opened. Senators were elected at the next federal electoral period under Velmora's existing electoral framework. The State's constitutional obligations — annual audits, Article I compliance, functioning electoral processes — began from the same date.

The Legislature could not have blocked this. No executive could have delayed it. A political coalition opposed to Velmora's Statehood had no constitutional mechanism to prevent it once the audit had passed. This was by design: the Statehood pathway is constitutional, not political. The criteria are objective. The auditors are independent. The outcome is automatic.

The significance is not just procedural. It means that a Territory cannot be denied Statehood by political opposition once it meets the constitutional criteria — and it means that meeting the criteria is the only path. There is no shortcut and no veto.
