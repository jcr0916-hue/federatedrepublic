Constitutional Founding · Article XIX · Four-Phase Transition · Ratification

The Concordance

Day Zero. Five founding polities ratify the Federated Republic simultaneously through a treaty of union. Phase 0 is complete. What the first 450 days of constitutional operation look like.

§19.1 The Activation Threshold§19.2 Day Zero§19.2 The Four-Phase Transition§19.2.b Phase 1§19.2.c Phase 2§19.2.d Phase 3

The Federated Republic was not created in a moment. It was created in 450 days — the length of the four-phase transition under Article XIX from Day Zero ratification to the first full Legislature convening and the first executives taking the constitutional oath. Day Zero was the beginning of a process, not the end of one.

Five founding polities had negotiated the treaty of union that the constitution's §19.1.c contemplates: multiple sovereign nations simultaneously pooling sovereignty through a treaty process, with the activation threshold met when the interim Elections Panel certified both that the NVS was operational and that the ratification threshold had been achieved. The NVS certification came first — a digital infrastructure requirement that the five polities had spent eighteen months building toward. The ratification threshold was achieved at 14:31 on the Day Zero morning.

Phase 0 Through Day Zero — §19.2.a

§19.2.a — Phase 0 Pre-Ratification Preparation
Phase 0 is the pre-ratification preparation period. The interim Elections Panel assembles initial candidate registers and establishes NVS infrastructure.

The joint Inspectorate panel formed during Phase 0 had assembled the initial candidate register for the MA board under §19.7. The NVS had been stress-tested across all five founding polities. The constitutional transition teams of each founding polity had mapped their existing institutions against the constitutional framework to identify conflicts and continuity obligations under §19.1.b.

Day Zero produced the Republic's first constitutional document: the interim Elections Panel's certification that both activation conditions had been met, published to the NRS at 14:31. The existing government officials of all five founding polities became caretakers under §19.3 at that moment — restricted to maintenance operations, unable to initiate new policy. The constitutional clock started.

Phase 1 — Days 1 Through 90

Emergency stabilisation. The Statehood Audits for all entering polities had been completed during Phase 0 — the LI's pre-activation audit of fiscal integrity and the JI's pre-activation audit of Article I compliance. Two of the five founding polities had passed both components cleanly. Two had conditional passes subject to remediation commitments. One had a partial failure in the Article I compliance audit: three specific legal frameworks that conflicted with §1.10 through §1.11 required legislative correction within the Phase 2 window.

The constitutional obligations that took effect on Day Zero under §19.5 were immediate: social insurance programs of the prior governments continued uninterrupted; active military operations continued under existing authorization; public debt obligations were assumed. The transition teams had prepared detailed continuity plans for each. The social insurance continuity was the most complex — five different existing systems, different benefit structures, different administrative frameworks, all now obligated to continue without interruption while the constitutional social state architecture was built.

Phase 2 — Days 91 Through 270

§19.2.c — Institutional Construction
Statehood Audits confirmed. First elections scheduled. Constitutional institutions constituted. Required agencies receive establishment timelines under §19.9.

The Phase 2 period produced the Republic's institutional skeleton. The Inspectorates were formally constituted — Inspector Generals selected through the transition processes. The NRS went live for public access. The NRS Panel and Elections Panel were seated. The CLT Administrator was established within the §19.9 Phase 4 + 180 day deadline — moved to Phase 2 because citizenship applications could not be accepted until the test was operational.

The polity with the partial Article I compliance failure enacted the three required legislative corrections on Day 187. The JI's compliance confirmation was published to the NRS on Day 194. The polity entered the Republic as a State rather than a Territory. All five founding polities achieved State status by Phase 2 end.

Phase 3 — Days 271 Through 450

The First Elections. The first National Election Day was held on Day 310. House elections in all States. Senate elections in all States. The SC's nine founding justices were nominated by the initial caretaker PM arrangement and confirmed by the initial caretaker Senate within the Phase 3 window under §4.2.h — all nine simultaneously, assigned to their staggered classes by public lot drawn by the joint Inspectorate panel. The NRS Panel and Elections Panel administrators were confirmed and took office, their staggered terms assigned by lot.

What This Scenario Tests

Article XIX's four-phase transition is designed to sequence the constitution's institutional requirements in order of urgency and prerequisite. Phase 1 stabilises what exists; Phase 2 builds what the constitution requires; Phase 3 elects the democratic institutions that give the new constitutional framework its legitimacy; Phase 4 begins full operation. The scenario demonstrates that constitutional founding is a process with a logic, not a single moment of creation.

The §19.5 obligations — social insurance continuity, military operations continuity, debt assumption — represent the constitution's recognition that the lives of citizens do not pause for constitutional transitions. People who depended on prior government social programs on Day Zero depend on them on Day One. The constitutional commitment to continuity is not a formality — it is the practical precondition for a transition that citizens can accept as legitimate.

The partial Article I compliance failure of one founding polity demonstrates that the transition process has teeth. A polity that failed the Statehood Audit entirely would have entered as a Territory rather than a State. The partial failure — specific legal frameworks requiring correction — produced a conditional entry with a Phase 2 correction deadline. The correction was made. The polity became a State. The system worked as designed.
