## Proposed sequence (order matters — the motion gates the government)

**The load-bearing dependency:** Thoss can't "form her government" until the §2.6 motion PASSES.
So the week opens by resolving the motion, then moves to formation. Don't publish formation beats
before the vote lands — it breaks the causality.

### Beat 1 — The motion resolves *(front burner, opens the week)*
The §2.6 vote. Does the Assembly majority hold? A constructive vote of no confidence is one motion
that both removes and installs — so the vote IS the installation. Show the arithmetic: who backed
the motion, who didn't, what the number was. **This is the piece that turns "filed" into "CC Thoss."**
- *Flag:* The World. Outlet: a Torenthian or RNN hard-news piece.

### Beat 2 — LC race, knocked sideways *(second burner)*
With the CC seat resolved by motion, the LC race (Vael/Carrow/Mak/Sandris) has to react. Does a
candidate drop? Reframe? Does the seizure change what the LC race is even *about*? This is the
"what happens to the race everyone was watching" beat.
- *Flag:* The World. Could be a reaction/analysis piece — a different outlet voice than Beat 1.

### Beat 3 — Thoss forms her government (I) *(front burner)*
Now she's CC. The first formation beat: the interesting *choices*. Who does an accidental,
motion-installed CC put in her Council of Ministers? A dark-horse CC has no coalition she campaigned
with — so every appointment is a signal. Highlight one or two that reveal the deals she made to get
the 60%+... wait — check the threshold: §2.6 is ABSOLUTE MAJORITY of full seated Assembly, names a
successor. So the deals are about assembling that majority. Show what she traded for it.
- *Flag:* The World. This is the meatiest piece of the week.

### Beat 4 — Thoss forms her government (II) *(front burner)*
The second formation beat: a specific consequential choice, or the first friction. Maybe a
ministry that reveals her actual agenda, or a faction she stiffed now grumbling. The "here's what
kind of CC she's going to be" beat.
- *Flag:* The World.

### Seeding — the Assembly Seat committee *(the quiet plant)*
An Assembly committee forms to study the §18.2 Seat of Government reselection requirements. Dry,
procedural, names no city, promises nothing. Just: "a committee will review the criteria and
process for the twenty-year reselection." The seed. Nobody thinks it matters yet.
- *Flag:* The World (or an NRS procedural record — even quieter). Best placed mid-week, buried
  under the Thoss noise, so the attentive reader finds it and the rest walk past.

### Mundanes (one/day, drawn from the simmering wells)
Ordinary-governance texture, not plot-advancing: a Rhondel ferry-schedule follow-up, an Argent
Ridge petition signature-count update, a routine NRS filing, a Corindal audit footnote, a
Lake Varda coast-guard log. These are the "the world keeps running" pieces — cheap to write,
they make the big beats land harder by contrast.

## Sequencing rules for the week

1. **Motion before government.** Beat 1 (vote) publishes before Beats 3–4 (formation). Non-negotiable
   — it's the causality.
2. **Vary the outlets.** Don't run four straight Torenthian hard-news pieces. Rotate: Torenthian for
   the vote, RNN or TNB for reaction, the Ledger for a human-scale angle, NRS for the procedural
   seed. The masthead variety IS the texture.
3. **Bury the seed.** The capital committee piece should publish on a busy Thoss day, not a quiet
   one — it's meant to be missed by most and caught by few.
4. **Respect the cadence.** ≤5 post-worthy/24h, target 3. A substantive beat + a mundane in a day
   is fine; two substantive beats + a mundane approaches the ceiling — space them.
5. **Check the constitution on every beat.** Especially the §2.6 threshold (absolute majority of
   full seated Assembly, names a successor) and the Council of Ministers formation rules. Verify
   against constitution_data.json before publishing, never from memory.
