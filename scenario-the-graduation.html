States & Territories · §15.2 · §15.3 · Mandatory Devolution and Restoration

The Graduation

Year 16. Valdrath passes its second consecutive clean Statehood Audit while in Provisional status. Under §15.1.a, Statehood is restored automatically — no political vote, no ratification, no executive action. Three years after the third audit failure sent it into Provisional status, the pathway back was the same one that went in: the audit itself.

§15.3 Mandatory Devolution §15.1.a Provisional Status §15.2 The Statehood Audit §9.1.a Legislative Monitor §9.1.c Judicial Monitor §3.5 The Senate

By Year 13, Valdrath had failed three consecutive Statehood Audits — persistent electoral district non-compliance in the LM's findings, Article I procedural gaps in the JM's. Under §15.3, three consecutive failures triggered mandatory entry into Provisional status automatically. No vote. No political deliberation. The LM published its third consecutive failure finding to the NRS, and Valdrath's status changed by constitutional operation the same day.

Provisional status under §15.1.a is not punishment, constitutionally speaking. Full self-governance continued: Valdrath's legislature, courts, and executive remained intact. Article I rights were uninterrupted. Social state access continued without change. The practical consequences were structural: no new Senate elections could be held while Provisional status was active. Valdrath's two sitting senators, elected in Year 11, completed their terms to Year 15 — and then those seats went dormant. Valdrath's Assembly seats continued on a proportional basis throughout.

The Provisional Audit Council — two CC officers, two Valdrath State delegates — convened and began publishing findings to the NRS. It had no enforcement authority. What it had was the record. Year 14's audit failed again — the same electoral district findings, the same procedural gaps. Year 14's Provisional Audit Council report named the deadlock in Valdrath's legislature directly. Year 15's audit passed both components: the LM certified fiscal integrity; the JM certified full Article I compliance. Year 16's audit passed both components again.

§15.1.a — Automatic Restoration
Two consecutive clean audits while in Provisional status. Statehood restored automatically. No political vote required.

Upon LM publication of Year 16's clean audit finding to the NRS, Valdrath's full Statehood was restored automatically. The same constitutional mechanism that triggered mandatory devolution — publication of an audit finding — triggered restoration. Entry and exit from Provisional status both work through the same audit machinery. There is no separate restoration vote, no legislative affirmation, no executive certification. The LM publishes. Status changes.

What changed instantly on restoration: Valdrath's Senate seat dormancy ended. New Senate elections could be scheduled at the next federal electoral period. Valdrath's two Senate seats, which had been suspended since Year 15, were open for election. Until senators were seated, the seats were vacant — not filled by interim appointment, not allocated to other States.

§15.3 — What the Senate Gap Looks Like
Sitting senators complete terms. No new elections during Provisional status. Seats go dormant, not vacant by reassignment.

Valdrath's Year 11 senators served through Year 15. When their terms ended, no replacement elections could be held — Provisional status was still active at that point. The Senate operated with 22 seated members rather than 24 from Year 15 to Year 16. All quorum and threshold calculations adjusted to reflect the actual seated membership, not the full constitutional complement.

This has a downstream consequence that §15.3 does not fully address: legislation passed during the gap required majority support from 22 senators, not 24. Senate votes that would have failed with a 24-member body might have passed with 22 — or vice versa. The constitution treats this as an inherent structural consequence of Provisional status, not an anomaly to correct. The remedy for a State that wants its Senate representation restored is the same as the remedy for entry: pass the audit.

§15.3 — The SC Petition Window
30 days to challenge the factual basis of devolution. Factual sufficiency only — not policy disagreement.

When Valdrath entered Provisional status in Year 13, it had 30 days to petition the SC on one ground only: that the audit findings were factually insufficient to establish three consecutive failures. The Court's review was limited to the factual basis — did the LM actually publish three consecutive failure findings? Not whether the findings were correct, not whether Valdrath's legislature had a good reason for the deadlock, not whether the threshold was fair. Valdrath did not petition. The findings were accurate. Three consecutive failures had been published.

The narrowness of the SC's review window is intentional. The constitution places the audit function in independent Monitors, not courts. The SC's role is to verify the constitutional trigger was correctly applied — not to second-guess the substance of Monitor findings. A State that disagrees with the content of an audit finding has one remedy: remediate and pass the next one.

Constitutional Finding

The pathway into Provisional status and the pathway out of it are the same mechanism — the Statehood Audit. Entry is automatic on three consecutive failures; restoration is automatic on two consecutive clean audits. Neither political opposition nor political support can alter either threshold. A legislature that wants to block a State's restoration cannot. A legislature that wants to prevent a State from entering Provisional status cannot — once the three failure threshold is crossed, the status change is constitutional, not legislative.

The Senate dormancy during Provisional status is the sharpest structural consequence, and the one most likely to motivate remediation. A State that values its Senate representation must pass the audit to get it back. The Provisional Audit Council's record — published to the NRS throughout — becomes the factual and political foundation for whatever accountability mechanisms the State's own citizens eventually apply to the legislature that caused the deadlock in the first place.

Valdrath's restoration in Year 16 produced no ceremony, no vote, no announcement beyond the LM's routine publication to the NRS. The constitution treats graduation from Provisional status the same way it treats entry: as a mechanical outcome of the audit. That is the point. The pathway is objective, automatic, and not subject to political management in either direction.
