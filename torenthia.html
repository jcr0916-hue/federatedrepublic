The scenarios elsewhere on this site test constitutional provisions against abstract situations. This is different. Torenthia is the Republic placed in the world — a specific nation, in a specific moment, facing specific pressures from real neighbors with real interests.

The constitution doesn't operate in a vacuum here. The Legat Consul has to decide whether to respond formally to Sunderland's naval movements on Lake Varda. The Civic Consul has to get the Legislature to appropriate emergency funding for Korda Territory's refugee infrastructure before the next audit cycle. The Monitors publish their findings whether or not anyone wants them to. The National Record System captures everything — permanently, publicly, unalterably.

Content grows over time. News articles. NRS entries. SC opinions. Diplomatic cables. Legislative records. Each piece is a constitutional moment made real — the framework operating as designed, or revealing where it wasn't designed for this.

The Republic occupies the center of a continent it did not choose. The Torenthian Alps run along the northern border with Caldris, and the Toren River drains eastward along their base — the Republic is downstream. To the east, Lake Varda is shared with Sunderland, its midpoint boundary disputed and increasingly tense. The Western Sea faces Marisvia across open water. Valedon presses against the southern border with eighteen years of unresolved political history.

Neighboring Sovereignties

The Nations Around It

Caldris
North & Northeast · ~85 million · Upstream Power
The Republic's largest neighbor and historically dominant regional power. Caldris controls the Toren River headwaters — the waterway that feeds Torenthian agriculture and industry downstream. The size differential shapes every interaction. Caldris remembers when the Republic's territory was within its sphere of influence. Its diplomatic establishment finds the Republic's constitutional sophistication alternately admirable and irritating.
Current tension: Caldris has proposed upstream dam infrastructure on the Toren River. The LC objected formally within 48 hours. The Senate passed a resolution directing minimum guaranteed flow thresholds. Treaty renewal negotiations are active. Caldris also holds economic investment in Sunderland that gives it interests in the eastern war the Republic cannot ignore.
Marisvia
West · Maritime Democracy · Across the Western Sea
The Republic's closest peer in constitutional values, arrived at through centuries of gradual parliamentary evolution rather than deliberate design from first principles. Marisvia's diplomats are sometimes quietly condescending about Torenthia's newness. Torenthian officials are sometimes quietly impatient with Marisian institutional inertia. They need each other too much to let it become a real problem.
Current tension: Marisvia had historical claims on Solara Territory and has never fully accepted the island's choice to join Torenthia. The maritime boundary is disputed. When Sunderland's collapse produces refugee flows westward by boat, Marisvia and Torenthia must coordinate a response — and they do not always agree on burden-sharing.
Valedon
South · ~25–30 million · The Nation That Almost Joined
Eighteen years ago, Valedon's citizens voted in a referendum to enter the Republic. The parliament rejected the result — citing procedural grounds, a threshold technicality, or raw political will depending on who tells the story. The pro-Republic faction does not merely want to join now. They believe they already voted to join and were denied. The government that rejected the referendum is mostly still in power.
Current tension: The question of whether a citizen referendum can constitute a constructive §15.6 petition from the governing body has never been put to the SC. The pro-Republic faction has grown since the eighteenth anniversary. Caldris was involved in the original rejection through back-channels — this has never been proven, and never been closed by the LC's intelligence services.
Sunderland
East · Authoritarian · Fracturing · At War
A nation in the process of coming apart. The authoritarian government is losing control of a significant internal region. The military has factional splits. Civil conflict has been producing refugees for two years, most crossing Lake Varda toward Korda Territory and the city of Rhondel. Sunderland naval vessels have been using the lake as a supply route.
Current tension: The LC has issued two formal diplomatic notes about Sunderland vessel movements in contested lake waters. The coast guard command in Rhondel operates under standing emergency protocols. Caldris holds economic investment in Sunderland and is not neutral. The relevant LC intelligence assessment is classified under §10.3. The EM has noted that the classification is approaching its constitutional ceiling.
Seronne
Southeast · ~5–8 million · Buffer State
Small, elegant under pressure, diplomatically sophisticated. Seronne has survived between larger powers for two centuries by being useful to everyone and threatening to no one. Its foreign policy is studied ambiguity — it hosts peace talks and issues careful statements. It has never joined a military alliance. Its northeastern corner touches Lake Varda, giving it a voice in any lake jurisdiction negotiation.
Current tension: When Sunderland's war intensified, Seronne's government made quiet inquiries about what §15.6 incorporation into the Republic might look like — not because they want to join, but because having the conversation signals to Sunderland that they have options. The Republic's quiet partnership with Seronne is one of the things that most irritates Caldris.
Solara Territory
Western Sea Island · Torenthian Territory · Distinct Culture
An island in the Western Sea between Torenthia and Marisvia. Distinct language (Solaran). Formerly had a state religion — solar-liturgical, disestablished either before or as a condition of joining. The oldest continuously inhabited settlement in Torenthian territory, Ostara, is Solara's ancient capital, built around a sun-court eight hundred years old. Solara chose to join the Republic as a Territory.
Current tension: The Statehood question is actively contested within Solara itself — the anti-Statehood faction fears assimilation. Solara has submitted a formal bid to host the seat of government under §18.2, which explicitly permits any State or Territory to compete. The bid is constitutional; the politics are complicated. Marisvia has never accepted the island's choice of Torenthia over Marisvia.
The Republic at Year 12

Twelve States · Four Territories

The Republic is twelve years old. Its institutions are established but not ancient. The annual Statehood Audit has been running long enough to fail twice in Corindal. The first 20-year seat of government review is active. The first generation of constitutional officers has turned over.

Year 12 Docket

Active Situations

Five things are in motion simultaneously at the opening of Year 12. Each one has constitutional dimensions. Each one is being documented in real time. Click a dot on the map above to open the situation brief.

⚠ Active Crisis
The Sunderland War — Korda Territory and Lake Varda
The Korda people entered the Republic as a Territory at founding under the indigenous sovereignty framework. Their ancestral land comprises two zones: a northern strip along the Torenthian Alps and Lake Varda's northern shore, and a southern zone adjacent to Kelvant. The northern zone bears the full weight of the Sunderland crisis — refugees crossing Lake Varda, Asylum Court overflow, Elections Panel direct administration last cycle. The southern zone is the subject of a separate constitutional petition. A Territory may consent to partial boundary adjustment — transferring a zone to an adjacent State — provided the remaining Territory remains viable for eventual Statehood. Kelvant's Governor, of Korda heritage, has petitioned for southern zone incorporation, arguing the northern zone is Korda's true heartland and separation strengthens both. The Korda territorial government counters that division permanently weakens the Statehood pathway. The JMC must assess northern zone viability before any transfer proceeds.

Ongoing · Unresolved
The Valedon Question — Referendum Wound, Year 18
Valedon's citizens voted to join the Republic eighteen years ago. The parliament rejected the result. The question has never been resolved. The pro-Republic faction has grown since the anniversary. A new civic organization operating legally under Valedon law has formally requested that the LC acknowledge the referendum result in any capacity. The LC declined without closing the door. Whether a citizen referendum can constitute a constructive §15.6 petition from the governing body has never been put to the SC. Several parties are waiting to see who moves first.

Ongoing · Treaty Renewal Active
The Caldris Water Negotiations — Toren River Treaty Renewal
The Northern River treaty is due for renewal. Caldris has proposed upstream dam infrastructure that would give it effective control over the Republic's downstream water supply in drought conditions. The LC objected formally within 48 hours. The EM published a fiscal risk assessment. The Senate passed a resolution directing the LC not to accept any agreement reducing guaranteed minimum flow below a defined threshold. The LC and the Senate do not agree on what that threshold is. Caldris's economic investment in Sunderland adds another dimension: Caldris is not a neutral party in the eastern war, and the water negotiation and the Sunderland situation are not separate files.

⚠ Provisional Status — Audit Failure
Corindal — Second Consecutive Audit Failure
Corindal has failed its Statehood Audit twice in a row for persistent electoral district non-compliance. The State is in Provisional status. The Provisional Audit Council — two CC officers and two Corindal State delegates — is active and publishing findings to the NRS. A forced devolution election was held and rejected by 71% of Corindal citizens. Year 14 audit is approaching. The audit failure is ultimately a political deadlock within Corindal's legislature that it has refused to break. The Audit Council's most recent report named the deadlock directly. The Council has no enforcement authority — but the record it is building does.

Active Review · Competing Bids
The Seat of Government Review — Verentum at Year 20
The 20-year seat of government review under §18.2 has produced three formal bids. Aldenmere defends on continuity: the institutions are here, the infrastructure is here, the memory is here. Orath challenges on democratic legitimacy: "Aldenmere was built around federal presence. Orath already exists independent of it." Solara Territory has submitted a bid on constitutional right — §18.2 explicitly permits any State or Territory to compete, and the Legislature selects by ordinary statute. Solara has Assembly seats but no Senate votes, which matters when both chambers must pass the enabling statute. Neither challenger can displace Aldenmere alone — together they might block retention, but cannot both win. The question nobody is discussing in public is which one yields first, and what they get for it.
