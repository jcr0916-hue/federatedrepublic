Neighboring Sovereignties

The Nations Around It

Caldris
North & Northeast · Upstream Power · ~85 million
The Republic's largest neighbor. Caldris controls the Toren River headwaters — Torenthia is downstream. It also holds economic investment in Sunderland, giving it interests in the eastern war the Republic cannot ignore.
Now: Proposed upstream dam infrastructure on the Toren. Treaty renewal active. The water talks and the Sunderland situation are not separate files.
Marisvia
West · Maritime Democracy · Across the Western Sea
The Republic's closest peer in constitutional values. They need each other — on trade, on Sunderland refugee coordination — more than either fully acknowledges.
Now: Historical claims on Solara Territory unresolved. Refugee burden-sharing from the eastern war is an active coordination problem.
Valedon
South · ~25–30 million · The Nation That Almost Joined
Eighteen years ago, Valedon's citizens voted to join the Republic. The parliament rejected the result. The pro-Republic faction believes they already voted to join and were denied.
Now: Whether a citizen referendum constitutes a constructive §15.6 petition has never been put to the SC. Several parties are waiting to see who moves first.
Sunderland
East · Authoritarian · Fracturing · At War
A nation coming apart. Civil conflict has produced refugees crossing Lake Varda for two years. Naval vessels have been using the lake as a supply route through contested waters.
Now: Three LC diplomatic notes on vessel incursions. Coast guard in Rhondel on standing emergency protocols. The classification ceiling on the LC's intelligence assessment is approaching.
Seronne
Southeast · ~5–8 million · Buffer State
Small, diplomatically sophisticated, deliberately ambiguous. Has survived between larger powers for two centuries by being useful to everyone. Its northwestern corner touches Lake Varda.
Now: Quiet inquiries about §15.6 incorporation — not because they want to join, but to signal to Sunderland that they have options.
Solara Territory
Western Sea Island · Distinct Culture · Torenthian Territory
An island with its own language and a sun-liturgical history. Chose to join the Republic as a Territory. The oldest continuously inhabited settlement in Torenthian territory, Ostara, is eight hundred years old.
Now: Statehood debate active within Solara itself. Has submitted a formal bid to host the seat of government under §18.2. Marisvia has never accepted the island's choice.
