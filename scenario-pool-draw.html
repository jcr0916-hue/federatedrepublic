JI Candidate Pool · Public Lottery · §9.4.a · §9.13

The Public Draw

The outgoing JI Inspector General's term ends in 14 months. The JIC conducts the public lottery from the certified JI Candidate Pool to select the successor. Three candidates challenge their pool exclusion. The draw proceeds.

§9.4.a JI Candidate Pool §9.13 Constitutional Pool Framework §9.14 Registry Integrity Standards §9.12 Joint Inspectorate Council §1.31 Continuity of Constitutionally Mandated Offices

The JI Candidate Pool is maintained by the Elections Panel and certified by the LI and EI — the JI has no role in constituting its own candidate pool. This separation is the design's first structural protection: the institution that will be audited by the JI IG cannot influence who enters the pool from which the IG is drawn.

Three former judges had been excluded from the pool before the draw. Each challenged their exclusion under §9.14: exclusion must be on stated objective grounds published to the NRS within 14 days, and the excluded applicant may petition the SC within 30 days of publication. All three petitioned. The SC reviews only whether the stated grounds correspond to an established eligibility criterion — it does not assess the factual merits of the disqualification decision.

§9.14 — SC Eligibility Review
Two reinstated. One exclusion upheld.

The first exclusion cited "judicial temperament concerns" — a category §9.14 explicitly prohibits. The SC reinstated the applicant within the 21-day review window. The second exclusion cited "ideological orientation" — equally prohibited. Reinstated. The third cited a documented financial conflict with a pending federal case — an established objective criterion. Upheld.

Both reinstated applicants entered the certified pool before the draw date. The Elections Panel updated the pool record to the NRS. The JIC confirmed the pool was certified and complete.

The Draw

The JIC conducted the lottery in a public session, with the outgoing JI IG absent. The draw was from the full certified pool — 23 former judges who met the objective eligibility criteria. The lottery result was published to the NRS the moment it was drawn. The selected judge, Adaeze Okonkwo-Mensah, had 11 years of judicial service and 3 years of pool membership. She accepted designation within 24 hours.

Under §1.31 and the statutory nomination timeline, the draw had occurred sufficiently in advance of the outgoing IG's term end. Okonkwo-Mensah would assume full JI IG authority at the precise moment her predecessor's term concluded — no gap, no acting period, no transition overlap.

What This Scenario Tests

The lottery is the JI IG selection mechanism precisely because it eliminates political debt. A JI IG confirmed by the Senate — even by supermajority — has navigated a political process. A JI IG selected by lot from a publicly certified pool owes nothing to anyone. The §9.14 eligibility challenge mechanism ensures the pool isn't manipulated before the draw: objective criteria, published exclusions, SC review of categorization only. The institution that administers the lottery has no stake in the outcome.
