The Judicial Pool — §4.2, §9.4.b
One unified pool feeds every federal judicial appointment. Entry requires 15 years of legal practice or 10 years of judicial service, no current government position, and no disqualifying financial interest — the same floor for an inferior court nominee and a Supreme Court nominee alike. The Elections Panel administers it; the JM audits it; the pool is publicly accessible on the NRS at all times.

When a Supreme Court Seat Opens Unexpectedly — §4.4, §4.4.a
STAGE 1 · 5 DAYS
Auto-Designation
Most senior Appellate judge becomes Temporary Associate Justice — full voting rights, no nomination needed.
→
NORMAL PATH
CC Nominates
Within 60–90 days, from the Judicial Pool. Senate must vote within 120 days — no deemed confirmation.

IF CC FAILS TO NOMINATE
Senate Direct Selection
CC misses the 30-day window after an LM finding of CC breach: Senate selects from the Pool directly, 2/3 vote — no further CC nomination needed.
or
IF SENATE FAILS TO CONFIRM
Public Confirmation
LM finds a Senate breach: CC + 2/3 Assembly may put the Temporary Justice on the next ballot — 60% of votes cast confirms.
How a Judge Leaves
Supreme Court Justice §4.3.b
Cause required→ Concurrent vote→ 2/3 both chambers
Three grounds only: constitutional breach, certified permanent incapacity (EM + LM jointly), or disqualifying conviction. Judicial disagreement is never grounds — independence of judgment is absolute.
Inferior Court Judge §4.2
Legislative/ Judicial discipline/ Criminal
Three independent tracks: legislative removal (2/3 both chambers), SC-overseen discipline process by statute, or criminal conviction. Policy disagreement is never cause on any track.
