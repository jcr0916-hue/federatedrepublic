# CONSTITUTION OF THE STATE OF ORATH

*A Free and Self-Governing State of the Federated Republic*

---

## PREAMBLE

We, the people of Orath, descend from those who first broke this ground — farmers, market-keepers, and the families who followed the rivers inland to build towns among the fields. That history taught us things we have not forgotten and do not intend to: that the people closest to the work know best how to govern it, that cooperation outlasts any single harvest, and that no office of this State — however senior — earns the right to stand apart from those it serves. We ordain and establish this Constitution to carry those convictions forward, for whatever Orath becomes.

We do so as a State of the Federated Republic, bound by and subordinate to the Republic's Constitution in all matters committed to it, and free to govern ourselves in all matters reserved to us. The land that shaped us remains central to who we are, and this Constitution protects it accordingly — but Orath is not only what grows from its soil. We write this Constitution for a State that may change its work across the generations without ever changing its character.

This Constitution binds Orath's government to the people of Orath, whoever they are and whatever brought them here — newly arrived or descended from those who broke this ground first. It is adopted, and may be amended, only by the people it governs.

---

## ARTICLE I — DECLARATION OF RIGHTS

### §1.1 Supremacy of the Federated Republic's Constitution
Nothing in this Article, or in this Constitution, may be construed to diminish, limit, or fall below any right guaranteed under Article I of the Federated Republic's Constitution. Where this Article is silent, the Republic's Constitution governs directly and of its own force. Where this Article speaks further than the Republic's Constitution, the greater protection applies within Orath. This Constitution is a floor beneath the Republic's floor, never a ceiling above it.

### §1.2 Inherent Rights
All persons within Orath are born free and equal, and hold inherent rights of life, liberty, the pursuit of a livelihood, and the enjoyment of the fruits of their own labor and industry. Government in Orath exists to secure these rights and derives its just powers from the consent of the governed.

### §1.3 Equality Before the Law
No law of this State may discriminate on the basis of race, ethnicity, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, religion, national origin, disability, age, or economic status, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.6. Orath additionally prohibits discrimination on the basis of **occupation**, so that no person may be denied a benefit, license, or public accommodation because they are a farmworker, tenant farmer, cooperative laborer, or seasonal employee.

### §1.4 Right to Water
Every person and every lawful enterprise in Orath holds a right of reasonable access to water for household use and productive use — agricultural use being, at present, the State's largest claim on that right, though this right belongs to any enterprise Orath's economy comes to depend on — administered through the water-rights framework established in Article X. This right may be regulated to ensure equitable distribution among users but may not be extinguished, sold in a manner that deprives smallholders of subsistence access, or withheld as a penalty unconnected to actual water use.

### §1.5 Right to Land and Agricultural Livelihood
Every person who has lawfully labored on agricultural land within a rural cooperative for five continuous years holds the right to petition for cooperative membership on terms no less favorable than those offered to any other applicant, consistent with Article V of this Constitution. No cooperative charter may categorically exclude petitioners on the basis of the characteristics enumerated in §1.3.

### §1.6 Labor Rights
All persons who labor in Orath — regardless of citizenship, residency status, or the nature or duration of their employment — hold the right to:
(a) a minimum wage no lower than that established by state statute;
(b) reasonable rest periods and safe working conditions;
(c) freedom to organize, bargain collectively, and withhold labor, consistent with §1.5 of the Republic's Constitution; and
(d) a forum for the resolution of labor disputes under Article IV of this Constitution.
No person may be compelled to labor involuntarily in Orath under any circumstance, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.4.

### §1.7 Right to Local Self-Government
The people of every municipality, county, and rural cooperative in Orath hold the right to elect their own local officials and to determine matters of local concern, subject only to the limits this Constitution and the laws of Orath establish. This right may not be abridged by the State except as provided in Article V.

### §1.8 Freedom of Movement and Settlement
No person lawfully present in Orath may be barred from settling, working, or traveling within the State on account of the characteristics enumerated in §1.3, nor may any county or municipality erect a barrier to entry inconsistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.8 (privacy) or §1.5 (movement).

### §1.9 Right to a Remedy
Every person in Orath may seek remedy in the courts of this State for any injury to person, property, or right, without unreasonable delay or expense, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.1. The General Assembly may not enact procedural rules that make this right illusory for agricultural workers of limited means; where private counsel is unavailable, the State shall provide it in proceedings before the Labor Arbitration Tribunals and the District Courts.

### §1.10 Environmental Stewardship
The State holds an obligation, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.18, not to cause environmental harm to the people of Orath through its own action or the actions of those it licenses — with particular, but not exclusive, attention to the air, water, and soil quality on which the health of Orath's communities depends, including the soil health and Toren tributary water quality that Orath's agricultural economy substantially relies on today. This right does not guarantee any particular environmental outcome but requires genuine and proportionate government action.

### §1.11 Rights Reserved
The enumeration of rights in this Article is not exhaustive. All rights guaranteed under Article I of the Republic's Constitution apply in full within Orath, whether or not restated here, and the specific mention of a right in this Article does not diminish any other right retained by the people.

## ARTICLE II — THE LEGISLATIVE BRANCH: THE ORATH GENERAL ASSEMBLY

### §2.1 Establishment
The legislative power of Orath is vested in a single house, **the Orath General Assembly**. Popular tradition still calls it "the Grange," after the agricultural cooperative movement that first organized Orath's farmers into a political voice generations ago; the name persists in speech, not in law, and this Constitution uses "the General Assembly" throughout so that the office outlasts the memory of any one industry. Orath adopts a unicameral structure in the judgment that a single chamber, directly accountable to the people and to strong local governments, best serves a State whose population and interests are not so diverse as to require a check of one legislative body against another; that check is instead supplied by the strength of local government under Article V and by the Steward's veto under Article III.

### §2.2 Composition
(a) The General Assembly consists of **87 members**, apportioned among districts drawn to reflect population as nearly as practicable, with weighting provisions to ensure rural districts are not structurally subordinated to urban population centers, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §3.3's prohibition on dilutive districting.
(b) **Fifty-eight (58)** members are elected from single-member districts.
(c) **Twenty-nine (29)** members are elected from a state-wide proportional list, to ensure minority political viewpoints obtain representation notwithstanding district boundaries.
(d) Members serve **four-year terms**, without term limit; provided that the General Assembly may by statute establish reasonable limits consistent with democratic renewal.

### §2.3 Districting
District maps shall be drawn by an independent Orath Districting Commission established by statute, applying the same criteria the Republic's Constitution imposes on States under §3.3: population equality, geographic contiguity, non-discrimination, no dilution of minority voting power, and reasonable compactness. Maps may not be drawn using political affiliation, voting history, incumbent residence, or racial composition as a primary criterion.

### §2.4 Elections and Eligibility
(a) All citizens and legal residents of Orath eighteen years of age or older, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §5.1's citizenship framework and Orath's extension of local voting rights to legal residents, may vote for General Assembly members representing their district.
(b) A candidate for the General Assembly must be a resident of the district sought for at least one year prior to election and must meet the disclosure requirements this Constitution and Orath statute establish.
(c) Federal voting rights, including the right to vote for federal office, remain governed exclusively by the Republic's Constitution and are unaffected by this section.

### §2.5 Officers
The General Assembly elects a **Speaker** from among its members by majority vote at the opening of each term. The Speaker presides over sessions, certifies enacted legislation to the National and State Record, and serves as Acting Steward under the conditions established in Article III.

### §2.6 Powers of the General Assembly
The General Assembly holds the legislative power of the State, including the power to:
(a) enact, amend, and repeal the statutes of Orath;
(b) levy taxes and appropriate funds, subject to Article IX;
(c) confirm or reject executive appointments requiring General Assembly confirmation;
(d) establish, alter, or abolish counties, municipalities, and rural cooperative charters, subject to Article V's protections;
(e) ratify or reject constitutional amendments proposed under Article XII;
(f) investigate the conduct of state officers and compel testimony and production of records relevant to a legitimate legislative purpose; and
(g) exercise all other legislative powers not reserved to local governments under Article V, not prohibited by this Constitution, and not preempted by the Republic's Constitution.

### §2.7 Legislative Process
(a) Every bill must address a single, clearly defined subject.
(b) No bill may become law until its full text has been published for public review for at least ten days before final passage.
(c) Bills pass by majority vote of members present and voting, except where this Constitution requires a higher threshold.
(d) Every enacted law shall be published to the Orath State Record and, where the subject matter touches federal compliance obligations, cross-referenced to the National Record System consistent with the Republic's Constitution §10.1.

### §2.8 Legislative Ethics
The General Assembly shall establish by statute an independent ethics body with authority to investigate and sanction members, consistent with the disclosure principles of the Republic's Constitution §12.4. No member may vote on a matter in which they hold an undisclosed direct and material financial interest.

### §2.9 Emergency Sessions
The Steward, or one-third of General Assembly members by written petition, may call an emergency session of the General Assembly. No emergency measure enacted under this section may derogate any right under Article I of this Constitution or Article I of the Republic's Constitution, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.19.

## ARTICLE III — THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH: THE STEWARD

### §3.1 Establishment and Title
The executive power of Orath is vested in a single officer, the **Steward of Orath**. Orath's use of the title "Steward" rather than "Governor" reflects the State's founding emphasis on stewardship of the land and of the office alike; it carries no lesser constitutional authority than the executive office of any other State.

### §3.2 Selection and Term
(a) The Steward is elected by the General Assembly from among its own sitting members, by majority vote, at the opening of each General Assembly term following a general election.
(b) The Steward serves a **four-year term**, coterminous with the General Assembly term that elected them, and may be re-elected **once** by a subsequent General Assembly.
(c) Upon election as Steward, a member vacates their General Assembly seat, which is filled as a district vacancy under §2.4. This mirrors, at the state level, the branch-separation principle of the Republic's Constitution §1.23: the Steward may not simultaneously hold a legislative vote.

### §3.3 Removal and Succession
(a) The General Assembly may remove the Steward only through a **constructive vote of no confidence** — a single vote that simultaneously removes the sitting Steward and installs a named successor — by a two-thirds majority of full seated membership. A vote that does not name a successor has no effect.
(b) Where the Steward is unable to serve due to death, resignation, or certified incapacity, the **Speaker of the General Assembly** serves as Acting Steward until the General Assembly elects a successor, which it must do within thirty days.
(c) An Acting Steward may not exercise the veto power under §3.5 but may perform all other functions of the office pending the General Assembly's election of a successor.

### §3.4 Powers and Duties
The Steward shall:
(a) faithfully execute the laws of Orath;
(b) appoint the heads of executive departments (including a Secretary of Agriculture, a Secretary of Labor, and a Secretary of Infrastructure), subject to General Assembly confirmation;
(c) prepare and submit an annual proposed budget to the General Assembly, which the General Assembly is not bound to adopt;
(d) grant clemency for offenses against state law, except in cases where the Steward was a direct participant or beneficiary of the offense;
(e) represent Orath in relations with other States, with rural cooperatives and municipalities under Article V, and with the federal government, subject always to the federal government's exclusive authority over foreign affairs, immigration, and interstate matters reserved to it under the Republic's Constitution; and
(f) declare a state of local emergency (drought, flood, labor disruption, or similar), which lapses automatically after thirty days unless renewed by General Assembly majority.

### §3.5 The Veto
(a) The Steward may veto any bill passed by the General Assembly by returning it, with written objections, within ten business days of passage.
(b) The General Assembly may override a veto by a **two-thirds majority** of full seated membership.
(c) A bill neither signed nor returned within ten business days becomes law without the Steward's signature.
(d) The veto may not be exercised against a bill implementing a constitutional obligation of this Constitution or the Republic's Constitution, nor against emergency legislation necessary to maintain compliance with the Republic's Constitution §15.2's Statehood Audit standards.

### §3.6 Limits on Executive Power
The Steward holds no authority to:
(a) suspend any provision of Article I of this Constitution;
(b) interfere with the sponsorship determinations of the Immigration and Labor Office established under Article VI in a manner that violates §1.3 of this Constitution or §1.6 of the Republic's Constitution;
(c) remove a sitting judge except through the process established in Article IV; or
(d) exercise any power reserved exclusively to the federal government under the Republic's Constitution.

## ARTICLE IV — THE JUDICIAL BRANCH

### §4.1 Judicial Power
The judicial power of Orath is vested in the **Orath High Court**, **District Courts**, and such **Labor Arbitration Tribunals** and other inferior courts as the General Assembly establishes by statute, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §3.14's recognition of the States' authority to govern themselves as self-governing communities, which this Constitution reads to include the authority to structure a State's own judiciary.

### §4.2 The Orath High Court
(a) The High Court consists of **seven justices**, appointed by the Steward and confirmed by the General Assembly by majority vote, serving **single, non-renewable twelve-year terms**.
(b) Justices must have at least fifteen years of legal practice or ten years of judicial service, and no disqualifying financial interest, standards consistent with those the Republic's Constitution applies to its own federal judiciary under §9.4.a.
(c) The High Court holds appellate jurisdiction over all District Court and Tribunal decisions, and original jurisdiction over disputes between counties, municipalities, and rural cooperatives arising under Article V.
(d) Any party may seek review before the Republic's Supreme Court where a matter presents a genuine question under the Republic's Constitution, consistent with §4.5's provision that a constitutional question arising in any state proceeding may be referred to the SC.

### §4.3 District Courts
District Courts hear civil and criminal matters arising under Orath law. Judges are elected by the voters of their district to **eight-year terms** and may be removed only for cause, on grounds of conduct, competence, or a disqualifying offense — the last term defined, for purposes of this Constitution, exactly as the Republic's Constitution defines it under §3.12.

### §4.4 Labor Arbitration Tribunals
(a) The General Assembly shall establish Labor Arbitration Tribunals to resolve disputes between agricultural employers (including rural cooperatives) and workers, including disputes over wages, working conditions, collective bargaining, and cooperative membership petitions under Article I, §1.5.
(b) Tribunal proceedings must provide the right to be heard, the right to present evidence, and the right to appeal an adverse decision to the appropriate District Court. No Tribunal decision is final until this right of appeal has been afforded, consistent with Article I, §1.9 of this Constitution.
(c) Tribunal members are appointed by the Steward with General Assembly confirmation for staggered six-year terms and may not be removed for the content of their decisions.

### §4.5 Judicial Independence
No public official may direct, threaten, or seek to influence a judge's or arbitrator's decision in a pending matter. Judicial compensation may not be reduced during a sitting judge's term. Removal of any judge or Tribunal member for cause requires published, written grounds and an opportunity to respond before the General Assembly.

### §4.6 Recognition of Judgments
Judgments of Orath's courts are entitled to recognition throughout the Republic consistent with the Republic's Constitution §4.6, and Orath recognizes the judgments of every other State and Territory on the same terms.

## ARTICLE V — LOCAL GOVERNMENT

### §5.1 Constitutional Status of Local Government
Consistent with the Republic's Constitution §15.8, the municipalities, counties, and rural cooperatives of Orath are the primary points of contact between the people and their government and are entitled to fiscal capacity reasonably sufficient to carry out their functions. The State of Orath may not systematically defund local governments to achieve de facto centralization of state functions, and any statute found by the High Court to have that purpose or effect is void.

### §5.2 Near-Parity of State and Local Power
Orath is founded on the principle that local governments are not mere administrative subdivisions of the State but co-equal partners in governance within their own domain. Accordingly:
(a) Matters of **purely local concern** — including local land use, local infrastructure, local licensing, and the internal governance of rural cooperatives — belong presumptively to local governments; the General Assembly may legislate in these areas only upon a finding, stated in the enacting legislation, that a state-wide interest requires uniformity.
(b) The General Assembly may establish **minimum standards** local governments must meet (e.g., minimum wage floors, environmental baselines, election administration standards) but may not direct local officials as mere instruments of state administration, mirroring the anti-commandeering principle of the Republic's Constitution §3.14 as applied between the Republic and its States.

### §5.3 Municipal Councils
(a) Every incorporated municipality in Orath is governed by an elected council, chosen by its residents under a charter the municipality adopts for itself, subject only to the requirements of this Article and general state election law.
(b) Councils may levy local property and business taxes, subject to the caps and non-discrimination requirements of Article IX, and may administer local services including housing, policing, and public health.

### §5.4 Rural Cooperatives
(a) A rural cooperative is a self-governing agricultural collective chartered under state law, holding authority over land leases, water allocation among its members, and labor coordination within its territory.
(b) Cooperatives are governed by councils elected by their members. No cooperative charter may be revoked by the State except upon a finding by the High Court that the cooperative has violated Article I of this Constitution or engaged in fraud upon its members.
(c) Cooperative profit levies, land-trust contributions, and internal governance rules are matters of cooperative self-governance, subject to the minimum standards the General Assembly establishes under §5.2(b) and the membership-petition right guaranteed in Article I, §1.5.

### §5.5 County Assemblies
County Assemblies coordinate between municipal councils and rural cooperatives within their territory on matters of shared concern — regional infrastructure, water-district administration, and emergency coordination — and are composed of delegates selected by the municipal councils and cooperative councils within the county.

### §5.6 Dispute Resolution
Disputes between local governments, or between a local government and the State, are resolved in the first instance by the appropriate District Court, with appeal to the Orath High Court, consistent with Article IV.

## ARTICLE VI — IMMIGRATION SPONSORSHIP AND LABOR

### §6.1 Recognition of the Two-Stage System
Orath acknowledges that legal residency in the Republic requires an applicant to clear two sequential stages established by the Republic's Constitution §6.1: **State sponsorship**, which lies within Orath's discretion, and **federal certification**, which lies exclusively with the Republic. Nothing in this Article grants Orath any power over the federal certification stage, over removal proceedings (which the Republic's Constitution §6.2 reserves exclusively to the federal government), or over asylum determinations (which the Republic's Constitution §6.3 places on an independent federal track).

### §6.2 The Orath Immigration and Labor Office
(a) There is established an **Immigration and Labor Office**, within the executive branch, responsible for administering Orath's State Sponsorship policy.
(b) The Office shall publish clear, objective sponsorship criteria in advance, consistent with Article I, §1.3 of this Constitution and §1.6 of the Republic's Constitution. Criteria may not reference nationality, ethnicity, or any characteristic enumerated in §1.3, and may not be applied so as to produce that effect in substance.

### §6.3 Sponsorship Policy
(a) Orath's default sponsorship policy favors applicants presenting a bona fide offer of agricultural employment within the State, in recognition of Orath's ongoing need for agricultural labor.
(b) The Office may not, and the General Assembly may not authorize it to, set an aggregate cap on the number of sponsorship applications Orath will process; such caps, where they exist, are exclusively a power of the **federal** Legislature under the Republic's Constitution §6.1, not a power available to any State.
(c) A denial of sponsorship is not reviewable by the Orath courts on its merits, but is reviewable where a petitioner alleges the denial was made on grounds prohibited by Article I, §1.3 of this Constitution, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §6.2(3). A court finding such a violation may order reconsideration free of the improper criterion but may not compel sponsorship.

### §6.4 Rights of Sponsored Workers and All Inhabitants
(a) A worker sponsored by Orath, once their application clears both constitutional stages, receives residency credentials issued ministerially by the Civic Consul; Orath holds no authority to condition, delay, or interfere with that issuance.
(b) All workers physically present in Orath — sponsored, awaiting certification, or otherwise — hold the labor rights guaranteed under Article I, §1.6 of this Constitution, without regard to the stage their application has reached.
(c) Orath imposes no obligation distinct from its general Article I duties toward asylum seekers, recognizing that asylum in the Republic operates on a constitutional track wholly independent of state sponsorship under the Republic's Constitution §6.3.

### §6.5 Local Integration Programs
Municipal councils and county assemblies may, at their own initiative and expense, establish housing assistance, language instruction, and job-placement programs for newly arrived workers and their families, consistent with Article I, §1.3's non-discrimination requirement.

## ARTICLE VII — ELECTIONS

### §7.1 State Elections
(a) Orath's state and local elections are administered by the **Orath Elections Office**, an independent body established by statute, insulated from removal at will by either the Steward or the General Assembly majority.
(b) All Orath elections must connect to and be conducted consistently with the National Voting System's technical standards where federal races appear on the same ballot, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §7.2 and §11.2, and no Orath statute or practice may reduce NVS access, functionality, or availability within the State.

### §7.2 Franchise
Every citizen and legal resident of Orath eighteen years of age or older may vote in state and local elections, subject to registration requirements that may not, in purpose or effect, make voting more difficult, less accessible, or less secure, consistent with Article I, §1.9 of the Republic's Constitution.

### §7.3 Federal Elections
Orath administers federal elections within its territory strictly according to the standards set by the Republic's Elections Panel and connects fully to the National Voting System, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §7.2. The Steward holds no authority to declare, postpone, or influence the scheduling of any federal election held within Orath.

### §7.4 Campaign Conduct
State and local candidates are subject to disclosure and conduct standards no less rigorous than those the Republic's Constitution establishes for federal candidates under Article VIII, including a prohibition on undisclosed contributions and a prohibition on foreign contributions to Orath campaigns.

## ARTICLE VIII — THE PUBLIC RECORD

### §8.1 Reliance on the National Record System
Orath maintains no record system separate from the National Record System established under Article X of the Federated Republic's Constitution. Every act of Orath's government required by this Constitution or by statute to be published is filed directly to the National Record System, in the section reserved to Orath, which this Constitution and Orath statute refer to as the **Orath State Record**. Orath builds no parallel registry, no independent archive, and no export or compatibility layer: what the National Record System already guarantees — permanence, public accessibility, and freedom from deletion or retroactive alteration — Orath relies upon directly, rather than reproducing it at State expense.

### §8.2 The Records Officer
(a) There is established the office of **Records Officer**, appointed by the Steward and confirmed by the General Assembly, serving a six-year term.
(b) The Records Officer's sole function is to ensure that every act of Orath's government required to be published under this Constitution or by statute is filed to the Orath State Record without delay, and to certify annually to the General Assembly that the State's filings are current and complete.
(c) The Records Officer holds no discretion to withhold, delay, or condition any required filing, and may be removed only for cause upon a finding by the Orath High Court that the Officer has failed to perform this duty.

### §8.3 Publication Requirement
Every enacted law, every executive order of the Steward, every General Assembly committee report this Constitution requires to be published, every disclosure required under Article II, §2.8, and every judicial or administrative decision this Constitution requires to be public, shall be filed to the Orath State Record no later than the period established by statute, which may not exceed thirty days from the act's effective date.

## ARTICLE IX — TAXATION AND FINANCE

### §9.1 Taxing Power
The General Assembly holds the exclusive power to levy state-wide taxes; municipal councils, county assemblies, and rural cooperatives hold the power to levy local taxes and assessments within their own jurisdictions, consistent with Article V. All taxation is subject to:
(a) the non-discrimination requirement of Article I, §1.3 of this Constitution and §1.6 of the Republic's Constitution;
(b) the prohibition on burdening interstate commerce more heavily than intrastate commerce, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §12.7; and
(c) the prohibition on taxing federal property or federal operational-use footprints within Orath, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §18.1.

### §9.2 Orath Revenue Fund
All state tax revenue is deposited into the **Orath Revenue Fund**, from which no expenditure may be made except by General Assembly appropriation. The Fund is audited annually by an independent state comptroller, whose reports are published in full to the Orath State Record.

### §9.3 No State Currency
Orath may not issue, and no county, municipality, or cooperative within Orath may issue, any instrument intended to function as legal tender, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §12.1.a's vesting of exclusive currency authority in the Monetary Authority.

### §9.4 Balanced Budget and Reserve
(a) The General Assembly shall enact a balanced budget each fiscal year or publish a written justification for any structural deficit.
(b) The General Assembly shall maintain an **Orath Agricultural Reserve Fund**, funded in years of surplus, to stabilize state revenue and local government support during crop failure, drought, or market collapse.

### §9.5 Fiscal Equalization
Orath is entitled to receive support under the Republic's Constitution §12.8's Fiscal Equalization mechanism where its own fiscal capacity is insufficient to meet its Social State and Article I obligations. Nothing in this Constitution obligates Orath to contribute to any federal reserve fund; such funds are maintained by federal surplus appropriation, not state contribution.

### §9.6 Local Fiscal Protection
Consistent with Article V, §5.1, the General Assembly may not enact a budget or tax reallocation with the purpose or substantial effect of depriving municipalities, counties, or cooperatives of the fiscal capacity Article V guarantees them.

## ARTICLE X — LAND, WATER, AND AGRICULTURE

### §10.1 Declaration of Purpose
Agriculture is, at the time of this Constitution's adoption, the largest part of Orath's economy and the origin of much of its political character. This Article establishes durable protections for the land and water that economy depends on, beyond what Article I states as individual rights — protections owed to farmers as workers and citizens are owed protection under this Constitution generally, not because farming is Orath's permanent or exclusive destiny. Should Orath's economy broaden or change across the generations, this Article's protections remain in force for whatever agriculture continues, and nothing in this Constitution requires the State's economic character to stay as it is today.

### §10.2 Water Administration
(a) The General Assembly shall establish a **Toren Tributary Water Board**, representative of upstream and downstream users, to allocate water rights among rural cooperatives, municipalities, and individual users in a manner consistent with Article I, §1.4's right of reasonable access.
(b) Water allocations may not be transferred in a manner that leaves a cooperative or municipality without reasonable subsistence access, and any such transfer is voidable by the High Court upon petition of an affected user.
(c) Disputes with other States over shared waters (including any dispute with Rhovane or Selvane over tributary flows) are resolved through interstate compact where possible and through the Republic's Supreme Court's original jurisdiction over interstate disputes where not, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §4.5.

### §10.3 Land Trusts
Each rural cooperative operating under Article V, §5.4 must allocate a portion of its profits, as the General Assembly sets by statute, to a **Community Land Trust** ensuring continued access to farmland for new and petitioning members under Article I, §1.5.

### §10.4 Agricultural Land Preservation
The General Assembly may establish, by statute, protections against the involuntary conversion of prime agricultural land to non-agricultural use, provided any such statute affords affected landowners just compensation consistent with Article I, §1.8 of the Republic's Constitution where a taking occurs.

## ARTICLE XI — EDUCATION

### §11.1 Right to Education
Every child in Orath holds the right to education adequate to enable full civic participation, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §1.17. The State shall provide free, compulsory public education and shall ensure that right is genuinely available and accessible — including to children in Orath's most sparsely populated counties, where distance and low population density make access harder to guarantee than in Dorven or any other population center.

### §11.2 Local Control of Schools
Consistent with the near-parity of state and local power established in Article V, every school district in Orath is governed by a locally elected school board, holding primary authority over curriculum, staffing, and the daily operation of its schools. The General Assembly may establish minimum academic and safety standards of state-wide application but may not direct a local school board as a mere instrument of state administration.

### §11.3 Rural Equity in Funding
The General Assembly shall establish, by statute, a funding formula that provides every school district a level of per-pupil resources adequate to meet the standards this Constitution and state law require, regardless of a district's population, property-tax base, or distance from Orath's population centers. No formula may be adopted or amended with the purpose or substantial effect of leaving a rural district persistently under-resourced relative to an urban one.

### §11.4 Higher Education and Technical Training
The State recognizes and may fund, charter, or provide land to institutions of higher learning and technical training, provided that admission and public funding comply with the Republic's Constitution §1.6. These institutions may include agricultural and technical extension programs among their offerings, but this Constitution does not limit the subjects the State's colleges, universities, and technical schools may teach, nor the industries they may serve.

## ARTICLE XII — AMENDMENT AND REVISION

### §12.1 Legislative Proposal
An amendment to this Constitution may be proposed by a **two-thirds vote** of the full seated General Assembly membership.

### §12.2 Citizen Initiative
An amendment may also be proposed by citizen petition, gathering signatures equal to **eight percent** of the votes cast in the most recent Steward election, distributed across at least half of Orath's counties to demonstrate genuine state-wide support.

### §12.3 Ratification
A proposed amendment, however initiated, takes effect only upon ratification by a **majority of votes cast** in a state-wide referendum, administered by the Orath Elections Office, with a participation floor of no less than thirty percent of eligible voters.

### §12.4 Limits on Amendment
No amendment to this Constitution may:
(a) reduce a right below the floor established by Article I of the Republic's Constitution;
(b) alter the near-parity of state and local power established in Article V in a manner inconsistent with the Republic's Constitution §15.8; or
(c) place Orath in conflict with any provision of the Republic's Constitution or with the Statehood Audit standards of §15.2.
Any amendment purporting to do so is void to the extent of the conflict, reviewable by the Orath High Court and, where a federal question is genuinely presented, by the Republic's Supreme Court.

## ARTICLE XIII — SUPREMACY, COMPLIANCE, AND CONSTRUCTION

### §13.1 Supremacy of the Republic's Constitution
This Constitution, and all statutes enacted under it, are subordinate to the Constitution of the Federated Republic. Where any provision of this Constitution conflicts with the Republic's Constitution, the Republic's Constitution governs, and the conflicting provision is void to the extent of the conflict, consistent with the Republic's Constitution §17.4.

### §13.2 Statehood Audit Compliance
Orath's continued status as a State of the Republic depends on its ongoing compliance with the Republic's Constitution §15.2, requiring functioning electoral processes, basic rule of law and judicial function, and Article I rights compliance, verified through annual audit. Every branch and every local government of Orath established under this Constitution bears an affirmative obligation to conduct itself so as to maintain that compliance, and the Steward shall report annually to the General Assembly on the State's standing.

### §13.3 Federal Preemption
Nothing in this Constitution may be construed to authorize any act by any officer or body of Orath in a field the Republic's Constitution commits exclusively to the federal government, including but not limited to foreign affairs, national defense, currency, federal citizenship and naturalization, and the federal certification stage of immigration.

### §13.4 Construction
This Constitution shall be construed liberally to effect its purposes: a strong, self-governing agricultural State, with local governments as genuine partners in power, operating in full and continuing compliance with the Constitution of the Federated Republic.

*Adopted by the people of Orath. Subject to ratification consistent with Article XII and to the ongoing compliance obligations of Article XIII.*
