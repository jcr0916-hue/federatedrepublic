# THE CONSTITUTION OF THE STATE OF CALDENMERE

*Adopted under the authority reserved to the States by the Constitution of the Federated Republic, Article III §3.14, and consistent with the Statehood Audit standard of Article XV §15.2.*

---

## PREAMBLE

We, the people of Caldenmere, heirs to the harbor and the shoreline, to the counting-houses and the fishing towns alike, in order to secure for ourselves and our posterity the blessings of a stable, prosperous, and self-governing state within the Federated Republic, do ordain and establish this Constitution.

We hold that government closest to the water knows the water best, and government closest to the town square knows the town best. We commit our State to the rule of law, to the dignity of every Inhabitant, and to the enduring principle that experience and judgment, freely earned and openly exercised, best serve the long continuance of our common home.

Nothing in this Constitution shall be construed to diminish, abridge, or conflict with any right, protection, or obligation established by the Constitution of the Federated Republic, which remains supreme.

---

## ARTICLE I — DECLARATION OF RIGHTS

*This Declaration supplements, and in no case diminishes, the rights guaranteed to every person under Article I of the Federated Republic Constitution. Where any provision of this Article is read to conflict with a federal right, the federal right controls.*

**§1.1 — Inherent Rights.** All persons within Caldenmere possess certain inherent rights, among which are the enjoyment of life and liberty, the acquisition and protection of property, and the pursuit of safety and prosperity. These rights are not granted by this Constitution; they are recognized by it.

**§1.2 — Equality Before State Law.** No law of this State, and no act of any State or municipal officer, may discriminate against any person on the basis of the characteristics enumerated in §1.6 of the Federated Republic Constitution. No statute, charter, or municipal ordinance of this State may establish property ownership, income, or net worth as a qualification for any public office, board, or commission. Distinctions in social standing, howsoever they arise in practice, confer no legal privilege and impose no legal disability.

**§1.3 — Freedom of Expression, Assembly, and the Press.** The rights of expression, assembly, petition, and a free press within Caldenmere are coextensive with §1.5 of the Federated Republic Constitution and may not be further restricted by State or municipal law.

**§1.4 — Right to Bear Arms.** Consistent with §1.10 of the Federated Republic Constitution, the State may establish licensing and safety frameworks for the keeping and bearing of arms, provided that lawful ownership for self-defense remains available to every citizen and legal resident.

**§1.5 — Searches, Seizures, and Maritime Inspection.** No person, vessel, home, or place of business within Caldenmere may be searched, and no person or property may be seized, without a warrant issued upon specific, articulable grounds by an independent Caldenmere court, except as the Federated Republic Constitution otherwise provides. Routine customs and safety inspection of vessels at the Port of Calden shall be governed by statute consistent with this section and may not be used as a pretext for a search of persons or cargo without individualized cause.

**§1.6 — Due Process and Access to Courts.** No person may be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law. Every person has the right to seek remedy for injury before an independent and impartial Caldenmere court, consistent with §1.1 of the Federated Republic Constitution. Where a person cannot afford counsel in a Common Pleas matter affecting liberty or subsistence, the Court shall appoint counsel at public expense.

**§1.7 — Right to a Jury.** In any criminal matter tried before the Court of Common Pleas, the accused holds the right to a trial by an impartial jury of the district, consistent with §1.12 and §1.13 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§1.8 — Property Rights and Just Compensation.** Private property may not be taken for public use without just compensation, determined by a process independent of the taking authority, consistent with §1.8 of the Federated Republic Constitution. Where the State or a municipality designates land as Strategic State Infrastructure under Article V of this Constitution, the affected owner holds the right to an independent valuation hearing before the High Chancery prior to any transfer of title.

**§1.9 — Freedom of Contract and Commerce.** The right to enter into lawful contracts, to engage in trade, and to pursue a lawful business or profession is protected. The State may regulate commerce for the public health, safety, and welfare, but may not impose a burden on out-of-state goods, services, or persons greater than that imposed on its own, consistent with §12.7 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§1.10 — Labor and Association.** Every worker holds the right to organize, to bargain collectively through representatives of their own choosing, and to engage in peaceable collective action, without retaliation by any employer licensed or chartered within this State.

**§1.11 — Stewardship of the Shoreline and Waters.** The State recognizes an obligation to the citizens of Caldenmere, present and future, to maintain the ecological integrity of its harbor, coastline, and territorial waters. No State or municipal action may permit environmental harm to a defined coastal community without a genuine, proportionate program of mitigation, consistent with §1.18 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§1.12 — Right of Local Petition.** Every resident of a municipality holds the right to petition their local governing body directly and to receive a public response within the period defined by municipal charter or, absent such charter provision, within sixty days.

**§1.13 — Non-Derogation.** No right enumerated in this Article may be suspended, limited, or derogated except as, and to the extent that, Article I §1.19 of the Federated Republic Constitution permits derogation of the corresponding federal right, and never below the federal non-derogable floor of §1.19.a.

## ARTICLE II — THE EXECUTIVE

**§2.1 — The Governor: Office and Eligibility.** The executive power of ceremonial representation and diplomatic liaison is vested in the Governor. To hold the office, a person must have been a citizen of the Federated Republic and a resident of Caldenmere for at least five years, and must meet the disclosure requirements of §7.4 of the Federated Republic Constitution as applied to state office by statute.

**§2.2 — Election and Term.** The Governor is elected by the qualified electors of the State at large for a term of four years, by ranked-choice ballot administered consistent with the National Voting System standards under Article XI of the Federated Republic Constitution. No person may serve more than two consecutive terms as Governor.

**§2.3 — Powers and Duties of the Governor.** The Governor shall:
(a) represent Caldenmere in all dealings with the Legat Consul, the Civic Consul, and the federal Assembly and Senate;
(b) sign or return bills passed by the Legislature, consistent with §3.3 of this Constitution;
(c) nominate members of the State Senate, subject to confirmation by the House of Districts;
(d) nominate State Ministers upon the recommendation of the President of the State Senate;
(e) grant clemency for State offenses, subject to the same prohibition on self-clemency and group amnesty established in §2.12 of the Federated Republic Constitution;
(f) call special sessions of the Legislature.

**§2.4 — The President of the State Senate: Office and Selection.** The administrative executive authority of the State is vested in the President of the State Senate, who is elected by the members of the State Senate from among their own number, to serve at the pleasure of that body. The President of the State Senate is a distinct office from any federal legislative officer, consistent with this Constitution's own non-confusion principle under §11.4, and no Caldenmere record may abbreviate the title in a manner creating ambiguity with a federal office.

**§2.5 — Powers and Duties of the President of the State Senate.** The President of the State Senate shall:
(a) set the legislative calendar and the order of business of the State Senate;
(b) direct and supervise the State Ministers in the execution of State policy;
(c) issue Administrative Decrees for the implementation of existing statute, which may not create new obligations or penalties not already authorized by statute;
(d) represent the State in ongoing technical and commercial negotiations, including with federal domain officers under Article II of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§2.6 — Succession.** Upon the death, resignation, or removal of the Governor, the President of the State Senate serves as Acting Governor until a successor is elected at the next general election or a special election called for that purpose, whichever the Legislature provides by statute. Upon the death, resignation, or removal of the President of the State Senate, the State Senate elects a successor from among its own members within thirty days.

**§2.7 — Removal.** The Governor may be removed by a two-thirds vote of the House of Districts and a two-thirds vote of the State Senate sitting jointly, upon grounds of conviction for a disqualifying offense as defined by §3.12 of the Federated Republic Constitution, or for demonstrated incapacity, or for constitutional breach. The President of the State Senate may be removed by a two-thirds vote of the State Senate alone.

**§2.8 — State Ministers.** The Governor shall appoint State Ministers to lead the administrative departments of the State — including, at minimum, Maritime Affairs, Treasury, Justice, and Interior — upon recommendation of the President of the State Senate. Ministers serve at the pleasure of the appointing authorities and require no further confirmation. Ministerial appointments, dismissals, and directives are published to the Caldenmere Registry.

## ARTICLE III — THE LEGISLATURE

**§3.1 — Composition.** The Legislature of Caldenmere consists of two chambers: the House of Districts and the State Senate.

**§3.2 — The House of Districts.** The House of Districts is composed of members elected by proportional representation from districts of substantially equal population, drawn consistent with the non-discrimination and anti-dilution principles of §3.3 of the Federated Republic Constitution. Members serve two-year terms. No member may serve more than six consecutive terms without sitting out one full electoral cycle.

**§3.3 — Legislative Process.** All bills originate in the House of Districts. A bill passed by the House of Districts is transmitted to the State Senate, which may approve, amend within the bill's subject matter, or veto it. Following passage by both chambers, a bill is transmitted to the Governor, who may sign it, allow it to become law without signature within ten business days, or return it with objections. A returned bill becomes law only upon a subsequent two-thirds vote of the House of Districts.

**§3.4 — The State Senate.** The State Senate is composed of members appointed for life by the Governor and confirmed by a majority of the House of Districts. Senators may be removed only for cause — conviction of a disqualifying offense, demonstrated incapacity, or constitutional breach — upon a two-thirds vote of the State Senate itself. No Senator may resign in favor of a designated successor; each vacancy is filled through the ordinary nomination and confirmation process.

**§3.5 — Powers of the State Senate.** The State Senate holds absolute veto authority over legislation passed by the House of Districts, final approval authority over the State budget, and the sole authority to elect the President of the State Senate from among its own members.

**§3.6 — The President of the State Senate presides** over sessions of the State Senate and casts a vote only to break a tie.

**§3.7 — The Sovereign Recourse (The Override Provision).**
(1) *Purpose.* To ensure that no institutional decision, however reasoned, remains entirely beyond the reach of the citizenry, this section provides a final legal avenue for the public to affirm a specific legislative will.
(2) *Scope.* This mechanism applies only to the override of a specific State Senate veto of a bill passed by the House of Districts. It does not remove Senators, does not amend this Constitution, and does not extend to any other State Senate action.
(3) *Phase I — Petition of Merit.* Following a State Senate veto, citizens may initiate a petition. To qualify for a vote, the petition must secure verified signatures equal to fifteen percent of the total registered electorate within sixty days of the veto.
(4) *Phase II — Popular Affirmation.* A qualifying petition triggers a state-wide referendum, administered consistent with National Voting System standards. The veto is overturned only upon a supermajority of sixty-five percent of votes cast.
(5) *Historical Note.* The threshold established by this section has never been met since its adoption. This is regarded by the State as evidence of the general alignment between the Senate's judgment and the public will, and not as evidence that the provision is without force.

**§3.8 — Ethics and Disclosure.** Members of both chambers shall disclose their financial interests consistent with the standard established in §12.4 of the Federated Republic Constitution. The Legislature shall maintain a joint ethics committee with authority to sanction members for conduct falling short of removal.

**§3.9 — Publication.** The final text of every enacted law, every State Senate veto, every Administrative Decree, and every disclosure required under this Article shall be published to the Caldenmere Registry, consistent with Article VIII of this Constitution.

## ARTICLE IV — THE JUDICIARY

**§4.1 — Judicial Power.** The judicial power of Caldenmere is vested in the Court of Common Pleas, the High Chancery, and the Court of Appeals of Caldenmere, subject at all times to the original and appellate jurisdiction of the Supreme Court of the Federated Republic under §4.1 and §4.5 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§4.2 — The Court of Common Pleas.** The Court of Common Pleas holds general jurisdiction over civil and criminal matters arising within the State, including matters affecting the personal rights of Inhabitants under Article I of this Constitution and Article I of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§4.3 — The High Chancery.** The High Chancery holds specialized jurisdiction over commercial disputes, maritime law, land title disputes, and matters arising from the Ministry of Maritime Affairs and the Calden Port Authority. Judgments of the High Chancery are subject to the same constitutional constraints as any other Caldenmere court and confer no exemption from Article I protections.

**§4.4 — The Court of Appeals of Caldenmere.** The Court of Appeals hears appeals from both the Court of Common Pleas and the High Chancery and is the court of last resort within the State on questions of State law. Any constitutional question arising in a Caldenmere proceeding may be referred to, or appealed to, the Supreme Court of the Federated Republic consistent with §4.5 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§4.5 — Judicial Selection.** Judges of the Court of Common Pleas and the High Chancery are nominated by the Governor from a Judicial Registry maintained consistent with objective, non-ideological criteria, and confirmed by a majority of the State Senate. Judges of the Court of Appeals are nominated by the Governor and confirmed by two-thirds of the State Senate. All judges serve single, non-renewable terms of twelve years and may be removed only for cause.

**§4.6 — Judicial Independence.** No public official may direct, threaten, or seek to influence the decision of a Caldenmere judge in any pending matter. Judicial compensation may not be reduced during a judge's term.

## ARTICLE V — LOCAL GOVERNMENT AND MUNICIPAL HOME RULE

**§5.1 — Municipal Sovereignty.** Every city, town, and municipal corporation within Caldenmere holds full and autonomous authority over matters of local concern, including zoning, municipal policing, local taxation, and local ordinance, except as this Article otherwise provides.

**§5.2 — Charter Government.** Any municipality may adopt a home-rule charter by majority vote of its qualified electors, establishing its own form of local government, provided the charter maintains democratic accountability and does not conflict with this Constitution or the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§5.3 — Strategic State Infrastructure.** State authority overrides municipal zoning and land-use authority only where the Legislature, by statute and upon recommendation of the relevant State Minister, formally designates a project as Strategic State Infrastructure. Any affected municipality or property owner holds the right to a hearing before the High Chancery under §1.8 of this Constitution prior to any transfer or override of local authority.

**§5.4 — Protection from Defunding.** The State may not systematically withhold fiscal support from municipalities for the purpose of centralizing State-level functions, consistent with §15.8 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§5.5 — Recall.** A municipality may, by its own charter, establish a recall process for local officials, provided the process is political rather than judicial in character and the final decision rests with the municipality's eligible voters.

---

## ARTICLE VI — FINANCE, TAXATION, AND ADMINISTRATION

**§6.1 — Taxing Power.** The Legislature holds the exclusive authority to levy State taxes, duties, and fees, subject to §12.3 and §12.7 of the Federated Republic Constitution. No tax may discriminate against goods, services, or persons of another State or Territory.

**§6.2 — The State Treasury.** All public money received by the State is deposited into the State Treasury and may be withdrawn only by legislative appropriation.

**§6.3 — The Budget Process.** The Governor and the President of the State Senate shall each submit a proposed budget to the House of Districts by the deadline established by statute. The House of Districts originates the appropriations bill; the State Senate reviews and may amend it within its subject matter.

**§6.4 — The Calden Port Authority.** The Legislature shall maintain the Calden Port Authority as the administrative body responsible for the management, development, and regulation of the Port of Calden, governed by a board appointed consistent with statute.

**§6.5 — Municipal Taxation.** Municipalities retain independent taxing authority within their own jurisdictions, subject to the non-discrimination requirements of Article I of this Constitution.

---

## ARTICLE VII — ELECTIONS

**§7.1 — State Elections.** All State elections — for Governor, House of Districts, and any local office electing to use State infrastructure — shall be conducted using ranked-choice or majority-vote methods as the Legislature provides by statute, and shall connect to and operate through the National Voting System established under Article XI of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§7.2 — Suffrage.** Every citizen of the Federated Republic who is a resident of Caldenmere and eighteen years of age or older holds the right to vote in State and municipal elections, consistent with §1.9 of the Federated Republic Constitution. No law, rule, or practice of this State may make voting more difficult, less accessible, or less secure.

**§7.3 — State Election Administration.** The Legislature shall establish an independent State Election Office to administer State and municipal elections within the standards set by the federal Elections Panel.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — THE CALDENMERE REGISTRY

**§8.1 — Establishment.** The State shall maintain the Caldenmere Registry as the permanent public record of State governmental action, equivalent in permanence and public accessibility to the National Record System established under Article X of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§8.2 — Publication Requirement.** Every State and municipal governmental act required to be published under this Constitution shall be entered into the Registry as a permanent record. Records may not be deleted or altered after publication; corrections are entered as new records alongside the original.

**§8.3 — Federal Interface.** The Registry shall maintain export protocols fully compatible with the National Record System, ensuring that all federally required disclosures are available to federal Monitors and auditors without delay or reformatting burden.

---

## ARTICLE IX — EDUCATION

**§9.1 — Public Education.** The State shall provide free, compulsory public education adequate to enable full civic participation, consistent with §1.17 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

**§9.2 — Higher Education.** The State recognizes and supports its chartered institutions of higher learning as instruments of the State's long-term prosperity and civic continuity, and may provide funding, land, or charter privileges to such institutions by statute, provided that admission and public funding comply with §1.6 of the Federated Republic Constitution.

---

## ARTICLE X — AMENDMENT

**§10.1 — Legislative Proposal.** An amendment to this Constitution may be proposed by a two-thirds vote of the House of Districts and a two-thirds vote of the State Senate.

**§10.2 — Ratification.** A proposed amendment takes effect upon ratification by a majority of the State's qualified electors voting in a referendum administered consistent with Article VII of this Constitution.

**§10.3 — Citizen Initiative.** Citizens may propose an amendment to this Constitution through the same two-phase petition process established in §3.7 of this Constitution, requiring signatures equal to twenty percent of the registered electorate in Phase One and passage by a supermajority of sixty-five percent of votes cast in Phase Two.

**§10.4 — Federal Consistency.** No amendment to this Constitution may take effect if it conflicts with, or purports to diminish, any right, structure, or requirement of the Federated Republic Constitution. Any such amendment is void to the extent of the conflict.

---

## ARTICLE XI — RELATIONSHIP TO THE FEDERATED REPUBLIC

**§11.1 — Supremacy.** This Constitution and all laws enacted under it are subordinate to the Constitution of the Federated Republic. Where any provision of this Constitution conflicts with the Federated Republic Constitution, the federal Constitution controls.

**§11.2 — Statehood Audit Compliance.** The State shall maintain, on a continuing basis, functioning electoral processes, basic rule of law and judicial function, and full compliance with Article I of the Federated Republic Constitution, consistent with the requirements of §15.2 of that Constitution.

**§11.3 — Federal Property.** Any federal installation, facility, or use right sited within Caldenmere is a registered Operational Use Right under §18.1–§18.3 of the Federated Republic Constitution. No such use right transfers territorial sovereignty from the State, and each reverts automatically to the State upon the conclusion of the federal function it was established to serve.

**§11.4 — Federal Officer Non-Confusion.** No office established by this Constitution may bear a title identical to a federal constitutional office in a manner creating reasonable ambiguity between the two. Where such ambiguity would otherwise arise, the State office shall bear a distinguishing qualifier.

---

*This Constitution, upon ratification by the qualified electors of Caldenmere, is the supreme law of the State, subject always to the Constitution of the Federated Republic.*
