The Automatic Floor

Year 7. The budget deadline passes without an enacted budget — the first time. No announcement. No emergency. The interim automatic appropriation takes effect by constitutional operation at midnight. What continues. What stops.

§12.2 Budget and Fiscal Accountability§4.5 Constitutional Continuity§9.1.a Legislative Monitor§9.6 Monitor Funding Guarantee

The Year 7 budget negotiations collapsed on the last day of the constitutional window. The coalition government could not agree on infrastructure appropriations for the Varentis corridor; the budget cycle expired without a vote. It was the first time the Republic had reached a budget deadline without an enacted budget.

There was no announcement, no executive order, no legislative motion. The interim automatic appropriation took effect by constitutional operation at the moment the deadline passed. The Monetary Authority certified the prior fiscal year's spending levels, adjusted for inflation, and published the certified floor to the NRS by 7:00 AM the following morning.

§12.2 — What Continues
The constitution defines exactly what keeps running.

Under §4.5, these functions continued at prior-year levels: military operations under active legislative authorization; social state obligations — pensions, disability payments, healthcare coverage; Monitor operations; NRS availability; and electoral administration. Civil service compensation continued on the same schedule. Debt service obligations were met automatically. The government did not shut down.

What stopped: every new initiative. No new program could be launched, no existing program expanded. Discretionary appropriations that had expired lapsed without renewal. The Legislature's budget for its own operating expenses was not among the protected functions — that fell to the member pay withholding mechanism. From Day 2 of the impasse, no member of either chamber received their legislative salary.

§9.1.a — The LM's Role
The breach is published. The pressure is real.

The LM published a compliance notice at Day 1, certifying the budget deadline breach. It published daily status updates. The political cost of the impasse accumulated in public record. On Day 17, the coalition reached agreement on the Varentis corridor appropriation. A budget was enacted. Member pay was released retroactively to Day 2. The interim floor lapsed the moment the budget was signed.

The constitutional machinery had held the government together for 17 days without anyone being in charge of holding it together. §12.2 had worked exactly as designed: not preventing the impasse, but preventing the impasse from becoming a crisis.
