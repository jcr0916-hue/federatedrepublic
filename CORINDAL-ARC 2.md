# The Corindal Arc — Long-Game Storyline Framework
*A decade-plus background thread: a corporation that lost at the Supreme Court plays a patient, scrupulously-legal money game to reshape the Court that ruled against it. Dramatizes §8.1 (electoral finance) — tested not by someone breaking the rules but by someone obeying them perfectly and still bending the outcome.*

## THE ORIGIN (established canon — already published)
**SC-Y12-0091 — Corindal Industrial Partners v. NRS Panel** (torenthia-sc-001.html; reaction in news-020).
- Year 11: the Executive Monitor audited Corindal STATE's fiscal administration (the audit that led to Corindal State's Provisional status under §15.1). As supporting material, the EM lawfully published Corindal Industrial Partners' proprietary procurement contracts to the NRS under its §9.1.b audit-publication mandate.
- Corindal Industrial Partners sued to have those records REMOVED.
- The SC ruled NO: §10.1 permanence is ABSOLUTE for lawfully-published NRS records. Correct, annotate, add addenda — never remove. Commercial confidentiality creates no exception.
- Doran's dissent (per memory) raised whether the EM's publication was within its constitutional mandate — the thread the company will spend a decade trying to get re-litigated by a differently-composed Court.
- **The company lost, and the thing they wanted buried is permanent.** That permanence is the wound that drives everything.

## THE MOTIVE
Corindal doesn't want revenge — it wants a Court that reads the EM's publication mandate (§9.1.b) more narrowly, so future audits can't expose them the same way. They can't un-publish the old records (§10.1 is absolute). But they can try to shape a future Court that would narrow the mandate going forward. That requires new justices. New justices require a nominating Civic Consul and a confirming Senate — and, if the current Court won't budge, MORE justices.

## THE CONSTITUTIONAL SPINE (why every step is legal, and why it takes a decade)

### The constraint: §8.1 Electoral Finance
- Corporate, organizational, foreign contributions: **PROHIBITED.** (Corindal-the-company can give nothing.)
- Individual contributions: **ALLOWED**, capped by statute, fully disclosed to NRS. (Corindal's executives/owners, as individuals, can give — within caps, on the public record.)
- Independent expenditures: **ALLOWED**, same disclosure as contributions. (Spending *in support of* a candidate, NOT coordinated with them, is legal.)
- Coordinated expenditures: **treated as direct contributions** — i.e. the tripwire. Coordinate with a campaign and your "independent" spending becomes a prohibited corporate contribution.
- **The killer clause:** electoral finance is EXPLICITLY carved out of §1.5 speech protection AND associational protection. So there is NO free-speech challenge available. The money game is the ONLY game — the courthouse door for a finance challenge is constitutionally welded shut. This is WHY Corindal plays the long lawful game: it's the only one that exists.

### The chain (each link legal, the whole chain patient)
1. **Assembly district races** — Corindal has real presence in specific districts (Corindal State + wherever its operations sit). Its executives give individually (capped, disclosed); aligned interests run independent expenditures (disclosed, uncoordinated). Legal. Slow. It shifts a handful of districts over several cycles.
2. **The Assembly composition** — enough sympathetic district members, over enough cycles, shift the chamber's center of gravity.
3. **The Civic Consul** — the CC is ELECTED BY THE ASSEMBLY (§2.6). Shift the Assembly and you influence who can assemble a majority for CC.
4. **SC nominations** — the CC nominates SC justices from the Judicial Pool (§4.4), Senate confirms. A sympathetic CC nominates justices who read §9.1.b narrowly.
5. **THE ENDGAME LEVER — §4.3 Court expansion:** if the sitting 9-justice Court won't revisit, the Legislature "may increase the number of justices by statute requiring 2/3." A 2/3 legislature + a sympathetic CC = new seats filled with sympathetic justices. **2/3 is the hard finish line — which is exactly why the game takes a decade or more.**

### The moral engine (no cartoon villainy)
Everything Corindal does is LEGAL and DISCLOSED. The tension isn't law-breaking — it's the unsettling spectacle of the system working exactly as designed while patient, lawful money slowly bends the outcome. The reader's discomfort IS the point: is this corruption, or is this just participation? §8.1 was built to force everything into the open — and Corindal obliges, scrupulously, and it may not be enough. That's the constitutional question the arc poses without answering: does sunlight (mandatory disclosure) actually constrain patient money, or just document it?

## ARCHITECTURE FOR A DECADE-PLUS ARC (framework, NOT a fixed beat chain)
This arc is too long to pre-script. It's a FRAMEWORK to drop beats into opportunistically over years of real publishing — it should surface periodically, recede, intersect other threads, and never feel rushed. Think seasons, not scenes.

### The phases (each spans multiple in-world years; may interleave with everything else)
- **PHASE 0 — Dormancy (now).** The decision is published; the company is quiet. Occasional dry signals only: an NRS disclosure filing showing a Corindal executive's individual contribution; an independent-expenditure disclosure in a district race. Nothing announced. A very attentive reader connects dots across months. This is where the arc LIVES most of the time.
- **PHASE 1 — The pattern becomes visible.** A reporter (Vend? a financial desk?) notices the disclosures cluster — same donors, same districts, same posture, all lawful, all filed. A "is this a strategy or a coincidence?" piece. The company declines to comment or gives a scrupulously bland statement. No law broken; that's the story.
- **PHASE 2 — The districts shift.** Over cycles, a few Assembly seats flip toward Corindal-aligned members. Traceable in the record. The Territorial/finance-watcher voices notice the Assembly's center moving.
- **PHASE 3 — The CC question.** The shifted Assembly matters when a CC is chosen. Does the aligned bloc influence the CC election? (Intersects any live CC storyline — e.g. could connect to the Thoss/CC thread YEARS later, or a successor.)
- **PHASE 4 — The nomination / the expansion push.** Either a sympathetic CC nominates a justice who reads §9.1.b narrowly, OR (if the Court won't turn) the 2/3 expansion bill appears. The 2/3 threshold is the wall — the arc can live for years in "they're close but can't get to 2/3."
- **PHASE 5 — Resolution (open, possibly never fully "wins").** Maybe they get there. Maybe 2/3 is a wall they never clear and the arc is about the SYSTEM HOLDING under patient pressure. Maybe a Monitor or the EM audits the pattern and the disclosure regime does its job. DELIBERATELY UNDECIDED — the ending is a design choice for when we get there, and "the guardrails held" is as valid an ending as "money won."

### Beat types (the registers to rotate, same as other arcs)
- **NRS (dry):** individual-contribution disclosures, independent-expenditure filings, district results. The paper trail. Where the arc mostly lives — a sharp reader assembles the pattern the characters haven't named yet.
- **News (human):** the "is this a strategy?" pieces, district-race color, the bland corporate statement, eventually the expansion-bill fight.
- **Dispatch/analysis (Sollis-register):** the constitutional-theory voice asking the real question — does §8.1's sunlight actually constrain patient money, or just record it?

## KEY FACTS TO KEEP STRAIGHT (verify against live canon each time)
- SC: 9 justices, three staggered classes, single non-renewable 12-year terms (§4.3). Expansion by 2/3 statute (§4.3).
- SC justices nominated by CC from the Judicial Pool (§4.4), Senate confirms.
- CC elected by the Assembly (§2.6). So Assembly → CC → SC nominations is the chain.
- §8.1: corporate/org/foreign contributions prohibited; individual capped+disclosed; independent expenditures allowed+disclosed; coordinated = direct (prohibited). Electoral finance explicitly NOT speech/associational protected — no constitutional challenge available.
- §10.1 permanence is absolute (the origin wound; can't be undone, ever).
- Corindal STATE (Provisional status, §15.1) vs. Corindal Industrial Partners (the COMPANY) — related names, distinct entities. Keep them straight.
- Doran's dissent in SC-Y12-0091 = the thread the company wants re-litigated (whether the EM's §9.1.b publication was within mandate).
