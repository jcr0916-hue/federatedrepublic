All legislation originates in the Assembly. Every bill must address a single, clearly defined subject — unrelated riders nullify the entire act. The full bill text must be published to the NRS for the minimum exposure period before any final vote.

The Assembly votes. An absolute majority of the full seated membership — not merely those present — is required to pass.

The Senate must take up every Assembly-passed bill within the statutory period. It may pass, amend, decline, or allow Deemed Approval to trigger. Senate amendments must be germane — limited to the subject matter of the original bill. The LM publishes a germaneness analysis for any amended bill returned to the Assembly.

State Pathways

The constitutional paths between Territory and Statehood — entry via the Statehood Audit, voluntary exit through devolution, and involuntary devolution on repeated audit failure. States may pursue full independence through §15.9 — a two-stage process requiring a 60% State referendum and concurrent ratification by both chambers, followed by a negotiated transition agreement.

Step 1 — Entry Territory Status

A Territory operates under a Territorial Compact providing an elected local governing authority, full Article I protections, and access to social state systems. Territories hold proportional Assembly seats but no Senate seats.

§15.1 — Territorial Compact
Step 2 — Evaluation The Statehood Audit

Two components, both must pass: the LM audits fiscal integrity; the JM audits legal compliance with every Article I right. A Territory that fails receives published findings and may resubmit after addressing them — there is no scored Baseline and no fixed percentage threshold; the Legislature defines audit methodology and resubmission timelines by statute. A geographic subdivision of a Territory may petition for Statehood independently of the rest.

§15.2 — The Statehood Audit
Both audit components must pass; no political vote is required once they do
Step 3 — Provisional Period Provisional Membership Period

On passing the audit, the Territory enters a Provisional Membership Period of one to three years, as the Legislature defines. Clean completion results in automatic Statehood — no further vote required.

§15.2 — Provisional Membership Period
Final — Full Membership Statehood Confirmed

The State receives two Senate seats, retains proportional Assembly seats, and accesses social state systems under Article XII. Every State must then maintain three ongoing conditions — functioning elections, basic rule of law, and Article I compliance — verified by annual audit.

§15.2 — Statehood is automatic on clean completion of the Provisional Period
Step 1 — Democratic Authorization 60% State Referendum on the Principle

A State may voluntarily restructure its relationship with the Republic. The process begins with a State referendum achieving 60% approval of eligible voters on the principle of pursuing devolution.

§15.4 — Voluntary Devolution and Merger
Step 2 — Negotiation Restructuring Agreement

On approval, the State and the Legislature negotiate a restructuring agreement specifying asset and liability allocation, transition timeline, the constitutional status of successor jurisdictions, fiscal obligations to the Republic, and continuing obligations. It is published to the NRS and is legally binding.

§15.4 — Restructuring Agreement
Senate ratification by 2/3 majority required to finalize
Step 3 — Effect Territory Status

Social state protections and Article I rights continue uninterrupted through the transition. A separate, related path lets a Territory or part of one merge with a contiguous existing State — requiring a 60% referendum with at least 50% participation, the existing State's own approval, a joint LM-JM audit confirming the existing State's integrity is maintained, and Senate ratification by simple majority.

§15.4 — Effect of Devolution; Territory/State Merger
Step 1 — Three Consecutive Failures Repeated Audit Failure

A State enters Mandatory Devolution upon three consecutive annual Statehood Audit findings of failure published by the LM. After each failing audit, the LM publishes an Early Warning specifying compliance requirements, and the State must publish a Remediation Plan within the statutory period.

§15.3 — Mandatory Devolution
Step 2 — Automatic Entry Provisional Status — No Vote Required

After the third consecutive failure, the State automatically enters Provisional status. No citizen vote is required. Sitting senators complete their current elected terms — Senate seats are not vacated — but no new Senate elections may be held until Statehood is restored. Existing Assembly members complete their current terms.

§15.3 — Mandatory Devolution
Article I rights and social state protections continue without interruption throughout
Step 3 — Restoration Automatic Return to Statehood

Restoration to Statehood is automatic upon passing the Statehood Audit and completing the Provisional Membership Period — no political vote required. The State continues under the annual audit cycle; any passing audit triggers the restoration pathway immediately.

§15.3 — Restoration is automatic on passing the audit
Parallel — Judicial Review SC Petition Within 30 Days

Within 30 days of Provisional status taking effect, the affected State may petition the Supreme Court on the sole ground that the audit findings are factually insufficient to establish three consecutive failures. The Court reviews only the factual basis and must rule within 14 days. This is the only avenue for challenging the devolution finding.

§15.3 — Judicial Review

Two Paths to Residency

The Republic has two constitutionally distinct immigration tracks. They operate independently and both lead to the same federal residency credential — but through entirely different constitutional foundations.

Applicant

Any person seeking to reside in the Republic permanently

Regular Immigration · Two-Stage System
State Sponsorship
State Sponsorship
A sponsoring State certifies the applicant through its own vetting process. States retain discretion over whom they sponsor but may not apply criteria that violate §1.6. The Legislature may also establish a Federal Sponsorship Category, where federal agencies serve as the sponsorship stage for applicants serving a defined federal interest — this does not bypass federal certification.
§6.1 — The Two-Stage Immigration System
Genuine Connection Requirement Applicant must maintain a genuine connection to the sponsoring State during processing. Credentials are federal once issued — not tied to the sponsoring State.

Key Constitutional Distinctions

Asylum is constitutionally independent The asylum track's foundation is §1.21, a non-derogable Article I right under §1.19.b. No legislation can extinguish the right to claim and have determined an asylum claim. The immigration pathway cannot be used to block asylum proceedings.
Credentials are always federal Whether granted through the two-stage immigration process or the Asylum Court, residency credentials are issued by the federal government and are valid in every State. No State may create a separate residency tier.
Aggregate limits apply to the sponsorship/certification track only The Legislature may set aggregate limits on immigration applications processed — these apply to the two-stage immigration track. Asylum proceedings are not subject to aggregate caps. · §6.1
Discrimination is always reviewable §1.6 non-discrimination applies throughout both tracks. State sponsorship denials are not subject to federal review on the merits, but §1.6 discrimination claims always have a federal court pathway. · §6.2

Military Authorization Framework

Military force operates under two authorized purposes and two authorization paths. The Legat Consul may act immediately in defense — but all sustained operations require legislative authorization or an existing treaty trigger, and run under continuous Senate oversight. Cessation is the constitutional default.

1
Immediate Defensive Force

Legat Consul acts immediately in response to an active or imminent attack on Republic territory, citizens abroad, or treaty allies.

Must notify both chambers and the Civic Consul within 24 hours, or sooner if statute sets a shorter period. Strictly bounded to defensive response.

§2.2 No prior authorization
↓
2
All Other Force — Legislative Authorization Required

Any military force beyond immediate defense requires legislative authorization stating which §14.1 authorized purpose it serves. Authorization not linked to a purpose is constitutionally void.

Cessation is the constitutional default. The burden is always on authorization, never on termination. Operations cease automatically on expiry.

§2.2 §14.1 Legislature defines timelines

Or: Treaty-Based Authorization

Where an existing treaty requires military action, the Legat Consul may act immediately — notifying both chambers and the Civic Consul within 12 hours and citing the specific treaty obligation triggered. Senate ratification of the treaty already constitutes legislative authorization; no separate vote is needed when its obligations are triggered. If the SC later finds the trigger conditions were not met, operations are limited to a defensive posture and both chambers must vote within 30 days on whether to authorize continuation — absent an affirmative vote, §14.2's expiration and wind-down rules apply.

§14.4 §3.6

Funding & Senate Oversight — Throughout All Operations

Military expenditure requires legislative authorization plus ordinary budget passage under §12.2; the Legat Consul executes the funding, bound by any categories or amounts the authorization specifies. The Legat Consul answers public Senate questions at statutory intervals and gives classified briefings during active operations — the Civic Consul is kept informed through the Council of Ministers, but holds no certification or veto over military funding. The EM audits expenditure throughout and publishes a full accountability report within 180 days of an operation's conclusion. On expiry or lapse, all expenditure ceases; existing contracts may wind down for up to 90 days with no new commitments.

§14.2 §2.5 §2.14

On Second Renewal: Transition & Reconstruction Plan

Once legislative authorization has been renewed a second time, the Legat Consul must submit a Transition and Reconstruction Plan to the Senate — covering alliance obligations, civilian reconstruction support, transitional justice, and withdrawal timeline. The Senate reviews and approves the Plan; the approved Plan binds both executives within their respective domains. Authorization may not be renewed again without an approved Plan.

Amending the Constitution

Three paths to constitutional amendment — parliamentary via State ratification, parliamentary via popular ratification, and citizen-led. No executive may veto an amendment. All amendments require genuine democratic consensus.

§17.2 — No Executive Veto Neither the Legat Consul nor the Civic Consul holds any veto authority over constitutional amendments. An amendment takes immediate effect the moment the LM or Elections Panel certifies ratification and publishes it to the NRS.

Institutional-Integrity Provisions Require Popular Ratification — Regardless of an amendment's origin, changes to the Monitor selection and independence mechanisms (§9.2–§9.4), the Monitor information-only mandate (§9.1), the Monitor funding guarantee (§9.10), the NRS architecture (§10.1), or Elections Panel/NRS Panel independence (§11.1) cannot rely on State Ratification alone — they must clear a popular vote. §9.6 — The Information-Only Mandate: What All Three Can and Cannot Do
✓ Publish facts to the NRS Every finding, report, and discrepancy notice is published permanently and freely accessible to all Inhabitants. No official may suppress, delay, classify, or alter a Report before publication.
✗ Issue binding orders Monitor findings are not legal determinations. They state observed facts. Courts, the Legislature, and the People decide what to do with them.
✓ Publish Annual State of the Republic Reports Every year, automatically, on a constitutionally fixed date — regardless of whether any official requests it. Content includes compliance rates, investigation summaries, constitutional strains, and a risk register.
✗ Arrest, prosecute, or enforce No arrest authority. No prosecutorial function. No enforcement power of any kind. Their sole constitutional output is published factual information.
✓ Receive minority reports from staff Any staff member may publish a Minority Report directly to the NRS if they believe a finding has been improperly omitted or suppressed. Staff cannot be disciplined for doing so — the protection is absolute.
✗ Classify their reports Monitor Reports are permanently exempt from classification. If an investigation involves classified material, the Report describes the issue's nature without reproducing classified content, and flags it for the relevant executive.

The Social State

The Republic's constitutional commitment that every Inhabitant is entitled to conditions of life consistent with human dignity — implemented through three mandatory systems and backstopped by the National Endowment.

System 1
Healthcare Insurance
Every Inhabitant enrolled from birth. No premium. No exclusions based on pre-existing conditions. Portable across all States.
§12.1
System 2
Social Assistance
Income floor ensuring every Inhabitant can meet basic material needs. The Legislature defines the framework by statute; the MA's fiscal integrity certification covers the system's funding adequacy.
§12.1
System 3
Compulsory Education
Free through secondary level. State-funded, constitutionally guaranteed. No child may be excluded on financial grounds.
§12.1
Funding Architecture
Normal Operation
All three systems funded through the ordinary annual budget. The Legislature appropriates; the MA certifies that the appropriation meets the constitutional dignity standard. If any system falls short, the MA publishes a compliance gap and the Legislature must address it.
Severe Revenue Contraction
When the MA certifies a Severe Revenue Contraction, the National Endowment activates automatically — disbursing from its constitutionally maintained reserve to backstop all three systems. Emergency reduction of social state access is constitutionally prohibited. §12.1
Monetary Authority Certification Role
The MA independently certifies: (1) that healthcare insurance is financially solvent; (2) that social assistance meets the constitutionally required adequacy standard; (3) whether a Severe Revenue Contraction exists that triggers Endowment activation. The MA cannot be directed by either executive in its certification function — these findings are constitutional facts, not political judgments.

Supreme Court — Appointment Process

Justices are nominated by the Civic Consul from the Judicial Pool — maintained by the Elections Panel, audited by the JM — and confirmed by the Senate by 2/3 supermajority. Nine justices serve staggered 12-year non-renewable terms.

Open Pool No political branch controls who enters the pool. Any citizen with 15+ years legal practice or 10+ years judicial service, holding no current government position and no disqualifying financial interest, may apply directly to the Elections Panel. The pool is public, permanent, and freely accessible on the NRS.
JM Audits, Doesn't Gatekeep The JM's ongoing audit of the Elections Panel's pool administration is published to the NRS, and the Senate may consider any published findings on a nominee before confirmation. The JM holds no role in constituting or certifying the pool, and no veto over nomination or confirmation.
Civic Consul Nomination + 2/3 Senate Confirmation The Civic Consul nominates from the Elections Panel-maintained pool. The Senate confirms by 2/3 supermajority of full seated membership. The 2/3 threshold requires broad Senate consensus — the Civic Consul cannot pack the Court without extraordinary legislative support.

Mid-Term Vacancies and Special Provisions
Mid-Term Vacancy — Special Nomination and Confirmation Death, resignation, removal, or expulsion triggers the §4.4 appointment process: Civic Consul nominates within 60 days (vs. 90 days for an anticipated vacancy); Senate must vote within 120 days of nomination, the same window as any other vacancy. Emergency Panel — Immediate Defensive Authority Scope Four justices drawn by rotation hold sole jurisdiction over constitutional challenges to immediate defensive military authority under §2.2 during active operations, and must rule within a period defined by statute, not to exceed 48 hours. The Constitutional Pool Framework

Eight constitutional pools operate through the same framework under §9.12. The table shows each pool across five dimensions: who fills it, what qualifications are required, who vets applicants, how someone is chosen from the pool, and what confirmation is needed. The Elections Panel maintains all pools administratively; the Elections Panel applies qualification criteria and administers pool entry; Monitors audit EP administration.

1
Entry
Who may join the pool? Self-nominated, State-designated, or executive-nominated. Never requires Senate confirmation.
2
Certification
JM reviews and publishes a qualification report within 30 days. EM reviews financial disclosure for offices with significant financial authority.
3
Selection
JMC public lottery (independence-critical roles) or deliberate nomination from pool (accountability-critical roles). All draws publicly certified and NRS-published.
4
Confirmation
Where required, Senate confirms the selected individual only — never pool entry. Some pools require no confirmation at all.

States & Federation

The Republic's constitution gives States significant autonomy — but within explicit, enforceable limits. This diagram maps what States control, what the federal government controls, and the contested boundary where they interact.

State Authority
State Sponsorship
States sponsor applicants at their own discretion — but not on §1.6 discriminatory grounds. §6.1
Assembly District Maps
States draw their own maps subject to five constitutional criteria; LM reviews for compliance. §3.3
Senator Recall
States may establish recall mechanisms by their own law; the Republic treats a recalled seat as vacant. §3.5
State Court Systems
States may maintain their own court structures; constitutional questions always escalate to federal courts. §4.1
Electoral Frameworks
States may offer early voting, mail-in ballots, and alternative methods — as long as all connect to the NVS. §7.3
Voluntary Devolution
A State may restructure its relationship with the Republic through referendum and Senate ratification. §15.4
The Boundary
Redistricting
State draws; LM reviews; Elections Panel draws an interim map where the State's map fails. §3.3
Immigration Sponsorship
State controls State sponsorship stage; federal government controls certification and credential issuance. §6.1–6.2
Statehood Audit
LM audits fiscal integrity; JM audits Article I compliance. Pass both → automatic Statehood. §15.2
Federal Property
Federal government holds functional use rights over a registered footprint; State retains sovereignty beyond it. §18.1
Amendment Ratification
Path A amendments require 2/3 of States to ratify; States hold direct constitutional power over amendment. §17.1
Federal Authority
Removal from the Republic
Only the federal government may remove individuals. State law cannot authorize removal. §6.2
Residency Credentials
Federal credentials are valid throughout the Republic; no State may create a secondary residency tier. §6.1
NVS Administration
No State may reduce NVS access, functionality, or availability within its territory. §7.3, §11.2
Article I Rights
Article I rights apply in full within all State territory. State law cannot abridge them. Article I
Territorial Integrity
A State may not secede by ordinary statute or State referendum alone. §15.7
Local Government Protection
States may not systematically defund local governments to achieve de facto centralization. §15.8

What States Cannot Do

Set immigration policy or conduct removal proceedings §6.1–6.2
Apply State sponsorship criteria that violate §1.6 §6.1
Reduce NVS access or impose additional voting burdens §7.3
Draw district maps using partisan registration or racial composition as a primary criterion §3.3
Divide directly into two States — the portion must become a Territory first §15.7
Systematically defund local governments to centralize functions at State level §15.8
Abridge Article I rights within their territory for any reason Article I
Block asylum proceedings by refusing State sponsorship §6.3
