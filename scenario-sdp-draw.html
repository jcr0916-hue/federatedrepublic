State Designee Panel · §8.9 · JIC Lottery · §8.13

The Panel Draw

A For-Cause removal proceeding is initiated against a senior government official. The Joint Inspectorate Council constitutes the State Designee Panel by public lottery under §8.9 and §8.13.

§8.9 State Designee Panel §8.9.a State Designee Pool §8.9.d Panel Constitution by Lottery §8.12 Joint Inspectorate Council §8.13 Constitutional Pool Framework

The State Designee Panel exists for one purpose: to determine, before any qualifying For-Cause removal vote, whether the stated grounds for removal are substantiated within the enumerated constitutional categories for the subject office. It does not investigate. It does not judge credibility. It does not recommend. It answers a binary question — grounds substantiated or not — and publishes that finding to the NRS.

The proceeding that triggered this draw involved a senior official in the Domestic Affairs domain. The initiating body had transmitted the stated grounds and supporting documentary record to the JI. The five-day draw window under §8.9.d began on the day of transmission.

The Draw Session — §8.13 · §8.12

§8.9.d · §8.13 — Pool Constitution by JIC Lottery
The JIC conducts the draw in a single public session. Certified jointly by all three Inspector Generals. Published to NRS in real time.

The draw session convened on Day 4. The Joint Inspectorate Council — the three Inspector Generals — presided jointly. The combined national pool was drawn from all 42 State Designee Pools simultaneously. Each pool member's eligibility status had been confirmed by the LI in the 48 hours preceding the draw: no current federal position, no pending criminal proceedings, no current service on any active Panel.

The draw was conducted in real time and published to the NRS simultaneously. Citizens watching the NRS stream saw each name drawn as it was selected. The five drawn members and two alternates were notified within the hour. Under §8.13, drawn members must accept or decline within 48 hours; alternates fill declined seats in draw order.

Three of the five drawn members accepted within 24 hours. The fourth declined — a recently retired government lawyer who, on reflection, determined that the subject matter of the proceeding was too close to her prior professional work to be comfortable accepting service. The first alternate accepted within 12 hours of being notified. The fifth drawn member accepted on the morning of Day 2. The Panel was constituted at five members, four full draws and one alternate fill, all within the 48-hour window.

The 21-Day Review — §8.9.f

The Panel's 21-day review clock began on Day 5 — the day after full constitution was confirmed by the JI. The documentary record submitted by the initiating body ran to 847 pages. The Panel requested an additional documentary submission from the initiating body within the first seven days, as permitted under §8.9.f — three specific documents referenced in the stated grounds but not included in the initial submission.

§8.9.g — Scope Limitation
The Panel determines only whether stated grounds constitute at least one enumerated constitutional category. No independent investigation. No credibility assessment.

The Panel's scope is deliberately narrow. It reviews the documentary record submitted by the initiating body — only that record, only those documents. It does not interview witnesses, does not conduct its own research, does not assess whether the official actually committed the acts alleged. It asks: do the stated grounds, as documented in the submitted record, constitute at least one of the enumerated constitutional categories for For-Cause removal applicable to this office?

This narrowness is the Panel's constitutional design. The Panel is not a trial. It is a threshold filter. Its finding of substantiated or unsubstantiated shapes the political process that follows — but the removal vote itself, which requires the applicable constitutional threshold, is where the full political judgment is exercised.

On Day 19, the Panel published its binary finding to the NRS: grounds substantiated. The finding was accompanied by no elaboration, no recommendation, no opinion on the merits of removal. The initiating body could proceed to a For-Cause removal vote. The applicable constitutional voting threshold applied regardless of the finding. The official remained in office pending any such vote, subject to applicable duty suspension provisions.

What This Scenario Tests

§8.9 and §8.13 operate in sequence: the pool framework constitutes the Panel; the Panel performs its narrow constitutional function. The scenario demonstrates that the State Designee Panel's constitutional value lies precisely in what it does not do. It does not investigate, does not reach a full merits judgment, does not produce a recommendation. It answers the binary question the constitution assigns to it and publishes that answer.

The JIC's role in the draw — all three Inspector Generals certifying jointly — ensures no single Inspectorate controls the Panel's constitution. The real-time NRS publication ensures the process is visible from the moment of draw. The 48-hour acceptance window and the alternate mechanism ensure the Panel can be constituted within the five-day window without the process stalling on individual decisions.
